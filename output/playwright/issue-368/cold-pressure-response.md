Direct consequences

Challenge: The causal chain introduces an Archive power not present in the selected record. FAC-1 requires “a living witness [to] name the speaker”; consequence #7 says the Archive can “verify” that witness’s identity. Naming, witnessing, and Archive verification are three distinct mechanisms, but the packet does not establish their relationship.

Risk: Verification scarcity, witness brokerage, oath fraud, and the existing disposition for #7 may all rest on an unadmitted assumption that the Archive authenticates witnesses or speakers. If it does not, the pressure point may be contested naming rather than scarce verified identity.

Alternative: Candidate for steward review: treat the immediate pressure as competition over access to living namers and disputes about whether a name correctly identifies a speaker. Assumption: institutions may attempt authentication, but no mechanism or success rate is supplied.

Question: Does the Archive itself authenticate the living witness, authenticate the named speaker, do neither, or perform some other packet-unspecified test?

Adaptations

Challenge: “Witness-brokers sell verified names and protective anonymity” plausibly follows from scarce access, but “verified names” and “protective anonymity” pull in opposite directions unless the steward specifies who knows which identity and who is protected from whom.

Risk: The brokerage may become a generic information market rather than a consequence distinctive to noon replay, living witnesses, and the old empire.

Alternative: Candidates for steward review, all speculative: intermediaries could arrange a witness’s presence without disclosing their location; litigants could compete for custody or cooperation rather than purchase names; witnesses could strategically refuse, delay, or condition their naming. None is established by the packet.

Question: What exactly does a broker control—identity information, physical access before noon, witness consent, credible naming, or protection after participation?

Countermeasures

Challenge: “Oath fraud” is not yet mechanically defined. The Archive can replay wording but cannot establish sincerity or truth; that limitation supports disputes about meaning and honesty, but it does not by itself establish forged replay, false identity, corrupted provenance, or fraudulent testimony.

Risk: A permanent tribunal could be solving an unspecified fraud category. It also closely resembles the retracted “Oath Provenance Office and a counter-fraud bench,” making the current third-order material vulnerable to restoring the same institutional idea under different wording.

Alternative: Candidate for steward review: separate possible targets of scrutiny before choosing an institution—whether the namer is eligible, whether the speaker was correctly identified, whether the replayed words count as an oath, and whether the oath was sincere or true. Assumption: these disputes can be procedurally distinguished; the packet does not say how adjudication works.

Question: Which concrete fraud or dispute creates the tribunal, and how is that materially different from the retracted provenance office and counter-fraud bench?

Fossils

Challenge: The fourth-order memorial depends on “fraud trials,” while the third-order consequence uses those disputes to justify a “permanent tribunal.” Without an independent causal trigger, the tribunal and its later memorials risk supporting each other circularly.

Risk: “Permanent” and “protected civic memorials” may overstate duration and public meaning before the packet establishes the scale, recurrence, legitimacy, or eventual abandonment of the institution.

Alternative: Candidate for steward review: pressure-test several non-final institutional trajectories—temporary hearings that recur, an existing court absorbing the disputes, competing jurisdictions, or a tribunal that later loses authority. Assumption: alternative legal venues exist; the unavailable world kernel prevents confirmation.

Question: What evidence in the available world makes permanence more likely than periodic, temporary, absorbed, or contested adjudication?

Alternative: Candidate for steward review: distinguish domains with packet-supported effects from domains that remain genuinely quiet or unresolved, then add only causally specific proposals where the available world supports them. Assumptions and provenance gaps

Challenge: The chain currently relies on at least these unadmitted assumptions: that “living witness” means someone who witnessed the oath; that the Archive verifies identity; that verification is scarce; that brokers can commodify it; that replay uncertainty produces fraud rather than ordinary dispute; that fraud is recurrent enough to create a permanent tribunal; and that the tribunal later leaves protected memorials.

Risk: Because the world kernel is unavailable and no related-world records were selected, none of those assumptions can be checked against broader institutions, technology, ritual, naming customs, jurisdiction, or historical precedent.

Alternative: Candidate for steward review: explicitly retain each unsupported link as a question or proposed-only causal candidate until the steward supplies the missing world context. Do not treat the active disposition for #7 as resolving whether Archive verification exists.

Question: Which of these assumptions does the steward intend to establish, which should remain unresolved, and which should be removed from this propagation chain?
