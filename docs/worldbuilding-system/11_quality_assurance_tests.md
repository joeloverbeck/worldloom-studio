# 11. Quality Assurance Tests

Use these tests after adding major facts or revising a world.

Score each relevant test:

- 0 = fails;
- 1 = weak explanation;
- 2 = coherent enough;
- 3 = strong, generative, and visible in the world.

A major fact should not score 0 on any relevant test.

## 1. Capability diffusion test

If this fact enables a useful capability, why has it not spread everywhere it plausibly could?

Strong answers include cost, infrastructure, danger, training, monopoly, taboo, ecology, law, secrecy, unreliability, or recent discovery.

## 2. Hostile optimization test

What would a ruthless, competent enemy do with this fact?

If the answer is "win easily," the world must show that or explain why not.

## 3. Institutional response test

What institution regulates, monopolizes, ritualizes, suppresses, teaches, taxes, criminalizes, or normalizes this fact?

If none, why has no recurring problem demanded one?

## 4. Economic substitution test

What old labor, trade, profession, asset, or route becomes less valuable?

If nothing changes, the fact is probably less useful than stated.

## 5. Complementary demand test

What becomes more valuable because of this fact?

A real capability creates supporting industries.

## 6. Scarcity and price test

What is scarce, who controls it, and what price is paid?

"No price" requires massive world transformation or rejection.

## 7. Access bottleneck test

Who can use the fact and who cannot?

If access is narrow, explain enforcement or scarcity.
If access is broad, explain social transformation.

## 8. Countermeasure test

What defenses, laws, habits, tools, taboos, or tactics exist in response?

## 9. Daily-life residue test

Name five ordinary signs of the fact.

If ordinary people would be affected but leave no residue, the fact is under-propagated.

## 10. Historical residue test

If old, name the wars, reforms, disasters, holidays, ruins, legal precedents, or inherited traumas it caused.

If none, explain recency, secrecy, or locality.

## 11. Knowledge distribution test

Who knows the fact, who misunderstands it, who lies about it, and who has incentives to control knowledge?

## 12. Secret plausibility test

If secret, how many know, why have they not leaked, what evidence is suppressed, and what false explanation exists?

## 13. Faction differentiation test

Do different factions adapt differently, or does the world have one generic response?

## 14. Center-periphery test

How does the fact differ between capitals, frontiers, colonies, villages, ruins, courts, temples, and criminal spaces?

## 15. Class test

Who gets safe access? Who gets dangerous access? Who is excluded? Who performs maintenance? Who bears externalities?

## 16. Law versus practice test

What do official rules say, and what do people actually do?

## 17. Mystery boundary test

What remains unknown, and what effects are nevertheless consistent?

## 18. Aesthetic integrity test

Does this fact deepen the world’s intended identity, or does it make the world more generic?

## 19. Narrative pressure test

What story conflicts does the fact generate without requiring contrivance?

## 20. Game pressure test

What choices, costs, asymmetries, hidden information, or resource loops does the fact generate?

## 21. Frontloaded seed test

Was this fact introduced in the initial premise? If yes, did it receive the same propagation scrutiny as later facts?

## 22. Fallout-style capability test

Use for any fact like "raiders can reprogram robots."

Ask:

1. What exact capability is being demonstrated?
2. What tools, knowledge, infrastructure, and time are required?
3. Why can this group access it?
4. Why do better-organized groups not use it more?
5. Why does this not transform settlement defense?
6. What black markets arise?
7. What countermeasures are common?
8. What professions become valuable?
9. What historical disasters has this caused?
10. What visible residues exist in daily life?
11. What constraints make the fact possible without breaking the world?

## Pass conditions

### Green

No relevant score below 2. At least two tests score 3.

### Yellow

One or two weak areas, but repairs are identified.

### Red

Any relevant test scores 0, or the fact implies a different world than the one being presented.

A red fact must be constrained, localized, historicized, reinterpreted, branched, or rejected.
