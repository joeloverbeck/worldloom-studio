# 22. Glossary

This glossary is authoritative for shared vocabulary. Higher-tier doctrine still governs meaning, but lower-tier protocols, checklists, and templates should use these terms consistently. Entries are in alphabetical order.

## Action arena

A recurring situation where actors interact under rules, incentives, information limits, resources, and consequences.

## Actor

An entity capable of intention or meaningful action: person, group, institution, species, spirit, AI-like mind, deity, faction, household, corporation, cult, or swarm.

## Admission ledger

The lightweight batch record for minor-work facts: one row per fact holding the precise statement, scope, truth layer, status with tags, admission operation(s), and one consequence check. Rows follow the record lifecycle like any card and are promoted to full cards when a fact turns out to be load-bearing.

## Aesthetic residue

Visible, audible, linguistic, symbolic, material, tonal, recurrent, sacred, horrific, dreamlike, comic, or sensory evidence that a fact has shaped the world.

## Audit trail

The accumulated, unedited sequence of governance reports — propagation reports, contradiction reports, change proposals, decision records — that shows how canon changed. Reports are not rewritten after the fact; a correction is a new record. The audit trail answers "how did canon get here?"; living records answer "what is canon today?"

## Authority tier

A document's position in the package's decision order. Doctrine governs primitives; primitives and governance govern protocols; protocols govern checklists and templates.

## Black market

An illegal, unofficial, forbidden, stigmatized, or extra-legal market that forms when demand survives prohibition, exclusion, scarcity, corruption, monopoly, taboo, suppression, or institutional failure. Use `black market`, not `shadow market`, unless the world itself has an in-world term.

## Branch canon

A continuity where some facts diverge from another continuity while remaining governed rather than discarded.

## Canon

The set of facts treated as authoritative for a given continuity, branch, scope, and purpose.

## Canon debt

Named, scoped, unresolved work attached to an accepted or under-review fact: propagation debt, evidence debt, residue debt, branch debt, mystery debt, or aesthetic debt. Canon debt is allowed only when visible and scheduled.

## Canon fact

A fact the world must answer to.

## Canon status

The current standing of a claim in canon governance: proposed, under review, accepted, accepted with constraints, localized, contested, quarantined, branch-only, superseded, deprecated, or rejected. Status is separate from truth layer, constraint tag, decision operation, and repair operation.

## Capability

A repeatable ability, method, technology, power, ritual, skill, biological trait, or institutional permission that lets actors do something.

## Claim

A statement made by a source or actor. A claim may be true, false, mistaken, propaganda, rumor, myth, branch-specific, or unknown.

## Consequence

A change the world undergoes because a fact is true.

## Consequence mode

The dominant form by which a world proves that facts matter: material/institutional, mythic, fairy-tale, dream, comic, sacred, horror, lyrical-symbolic, or a declared hybrid. Consequence mode is not canon status, truth layer, constraint tag, decision operation, or repair operation.

## Constraint

A limit, bottleneck, cost, scope boundary, failure mode, taboo, rule, access condition, or dependency that shapes how a fact can operate.

## Constraint tag

A concise label describing how an accepted or reviewable fact is limited: local, rare, recent, secret, misunderstood, costly, dangerous, unstable, seasonal, jurisdictional, class-bound, model-limited, ritual-bound, geography-bound, branch-bound, and so on. Constraint tags coexist with statuses; they do not replace statuses.

## Cost

Anything paid, risked, consumed, delayed, damaged, forfeited, exposed, or morally compromised when a fact is used, known, hidden, enforced, resisted, or repeated. Cost may be monetary, labor, time, attention, bodily harm, social legitimacy, legal exposure, spiritual debt, ecological damage, memory, opportunity, relationship, identity, or aesthetic integrity.

## Counter-institution

An unofficial, rival, underground, illegal, informal, rebel, sacred, mutual-aid, or black-market structure created in response to official failure or suppression.

## Decision jurisdiction

The rule determining which operation family applies: canon status, constraint tag, admission decision operation, repair operation, branch decision, or truth-layer assignment.

## Decision operation

An admission-governance action applied to a proposal: accept, constrain, localize, historicize, reinterpret, institutionalize, price, branch, quarantine, supersede, deprecate, or reject. Decision operations answer “what do we do with this proposal?” They do not by themselves diagnose a contradiction.

## Diegetic claim

A claim made inside the world by a character, institution, text, tradition, rumor, or artifact.

## Domain

One of the fourteen world domains defined in `04_domain_atlas.md` through which facts propagate: physics/metaphysics/cosmology; geography/climate/infrastructure; ecology/food/disease/nonhuman life; population/demography/household life; production/labor/technology-magic; economy/trade/scarcity; governance/law/bureaucracy; war/coercion/security; religion/ritual/myth/meaning; culture/custom/language/identity; knowledge/education/science/records; history/memory/path dependence; daily life/material residue; aesthetics/tone/narrative use.

## Earned sacred opacity

A protected sacred unknown that refuses a specific explanation while accepting consequence, cost, recurrence, ritual boundary, contradiction repair, evidence handling, and craft critique. Earned sacred opacity protects the holy or numinous from reduction; it does not protect the steward from accountability.

## Evidence habitat

The social and material setting that lets a belief persist: source, institution, ritual, incentive, memory, punishment, identity, convenience, proof standard, or absence of accessible counter-evidence.

## Explanation pressure

The force exerted by QA, retcon, adaptation, audience curiosity, gameplay, or contributor convenience to turn an unknown or non-instrumental effect into a solved mechanism. Explanation pressure is not always bad; it must be governed.

## Fact admission

The process of deciding whether and how a proposed fact enters canon.

## Fact type

A practical classification used during admission: existence, capability, constraint, event, history, institution, relationship, resource, rule, exception, belief, mystery, wonder, sacred opacity, horror/dread, symbolic excess, aesthetic, branch.

## Fossil

A durable residue from an older fact or era: ruin, name, law, ritual, tool, border, scar, taboo, proverb, costume, office, inequality, myth, measurement, route, calendar, or dead institution.

## Governance

Human stewardship of canon: proposing, reviewing, accepting, constraining, branching, quarantining, repairing, retiring, and recording.

## Horror / terror / dread

Related but distinct pressure forms: horror confirms violation, terror anticipates danger, and dread sustains a reality-threatening unease. These can overlap with mystery but should not be solved away automatically.

## Institution

A stable pattern of rules-in-use, norms, roles, rights, obligations, sanctions, procedures, expectations, legitimacy, and enforcement. Institutions may be formal or informal.

## Invariant anchor

A fact, constraint, relationship, mystery boundary, tone, symbol, or ordinary-life pressure that must remain stable across branches or adaptations for the world to still feel like itself.

## Latency

The delay between a fact becoming true and the world adapting to it. Latency may arise from discovery, proof, travel, training, manufacturing, adoption, law, generation, taboo, suppression, ecology, or aesthetic reveal.

## Living record

A card — fact, capability, constraint, institution, or any other — maintained in the present tense and updated in place as canon changes. Outgoing wording moves to the card's history note with its retirement status (`superseded`, `deprecated`) and a pointer to the report that changed it. The set of live cards is the current state of canon.

## Localized fact

A fact accepted only within a defined place, group, route, jurisdiction, institution, species, era, class, ritual, metaphysical condition, or branch.

## Mode-aware consequence

The doctrine that every fact must produce consequence, but the form of consequence must fit the world’s declared mode. Institutional/economic consequence remains mandatory for institutional-realism worlds; recurrence, ritual logic, symbolic transformation, sacred opacity, dread, comedy, or symbolic excess may be the hard proof in non-naturalistic worlds.

## Mode-equivalent consequence

The specific consequence form a declared non-naturalistic mode substitutes for institutional or economic proof: recurrence, motif, taboo, ritual condition, transformation, comic reversal, associative dream return, dread ecology, or symbolic residue. Named at admission (`06`, Step 2A) and tested by the institution-or-mode-equivalent and value-pressure QA tests (`18`).

## Mystery boundary

The line protecting what is fixed, unknown, unknowable, author-secret, disputed, deliberately undecided, revealable, or forbidden to use. It is not identical to sacred opacity or wonder unless the protected effect also contains a puzzle-like unknown.

## Negative domain

A domain that appears unaffected by a fact even though causal pressure suggests it should be affected. Negative domains are contradiction candidates.

## Objective canon

What is true in the active continuity regardless of what characters believe.

## Opacity accountability test

The five-part stewardship test for protected sacred opacity: state the refused explanation, state fixed consequences/costs, state recurrence or boundary, state contradiction-handling implications, and state which craft critiques remain fair. It is applied in contradiction/mystery governance, aesthetic coherence, the mystery-preservation checklist, and the mystery-ledger template.

## Operation

A recorded action taken in governance. Admission uses decision operations; contradiction treatment uses repair operations. Always label the context.

## Path dependence

The way earlier facts, investments, institutions, roads, rituals, habits, traumas, classifications, or accidents make some later possibilities easier, harder, more legitimate, or nearly unthinkable. Path dependence is why the world is not freely redesigned after every new fact.

## Preservation boundary

A recorded limit on explanation, use, adaptation, reveal, measurement, mapping, pricing, or decoding that protects a mystery, wonder, sacred opacity, horror, dread, or symbolic excess. A preservation boundary is not a canon status or constraint tag, though it may be enforced by constraints.

## Pressure line

A causal path along which the world must expand because a fact creates dependencies, adaptations, costs, beliefs, institutions, or residues.

## Propagation

The process by which facts create consequences across domains, scales, times, places, actors, institutions, and aesthetics.

## Protected effect

An umbrella term for effects guarded from flattening explanation: a governed mystery, wonder/awe/sublimity, sacred opacity, horror/terror/dread, or symbolic excess. Protected effects are also called non-instrumental effects where their purpose is not to hide a solvable answer. They are recorded in the mystery/wonder ledger, guarded by preservation boundaries, and never excuse missing consequence.

## Public belief

What a broad population believes, whether or not it is objectively true.

## Quarantine

A status or operation preventing a proposal or fact from active use until contradictions, implications, dependencies, or mystery risks are resolved. Quarantine is controlled suspension, not rejection.

## Recurrence consequence

A repeated motif, rule, event, transformation, number, threshold, joke, dream, omen, or ritual return that carries causal force.

## Regression profile

A QA output naming the strongest and weakest parts of a world section, its most dangerous under-propagated fact, fragile mystery, overloaded constraint, branch risk, and unresolved canon debt.

## Repair operation

A contradiction- or instability-treatment action: clarify scope, add constraint, price, localize, historicize, institutionalize, diffuse unevenly, reinterpret, split, retcon, quarantine, or reject. Repair operations answer “how do we treat this incompatibility?” They do not by themselves grant broad canon standing.

## Residue

Observable evidence that a fact has shaped the world: habit, scar, law, price, ruin, joke, taboo, route, profession, dialect, ritual, tool, architecture, body practice, record, absence, negative space, or aesthetic mark. A fossil is old or durable residue.

## Retcon

A governed change in how earlier canon is interpreted, constrained, replaced, branched, or retired. A retcon must pay continuity, institutional, character, mystery, aesthetic, and future-use costs.

## Ritual logic

A pattern in which rites, taboos, thresholds, exchanges, sacrifices, blessings, curses, or ceremonial conditions determine consequence.

## Rules-in-use

The rules people actually follow, enforce, evade, bargain with, or fear, whether or not those rules match formal law.

## Sacred opacity

Protected unknowability whose refusal of ordinary use, pricing, naming, measuring, mapping, or explanation is part of its sacred force.

## Sacred-opacity cheat

A misuse of sacred opacity in which “it is sacred” is used to escape consequence, hide vagueness, dodge contradiction repair, cancel cost, block audience-facing clarity, or shield a favored image from criticism. A sacred-opacity cheat is canon debt until repaired or rejected.

## Shock cone

The widening pattern of consequences produced when a fact enters the world: immediate effects, prerequisites exposed, domain ripples, institutional adaptation, second-order adaptations, fossils, countermeasures, and distant echoes.

## Source

A person, institution, artifact, tradition, record, sensor, ritual, memory, document, witness, performance, map, price movement, or absence that supports a claim.

## Status

See canon status.

## Suppression

The deliberate distortion, concealment, criminalization, monopolization, erasure, or weaponization of a fact, claim, source, evidence, language, record, institution, route, or memory.

## Symbolic excess

Meaning that exceeds one-to-one function or decoding while still exerting consistent pressure through motif, image, language, ritual, gesture, material, or recurrence.

## Symbolic transformation

A change in body, object, animal, weather, architecture, language, color, sound, or social sign by which meaning becomes worldly pressure.

## Thin-start rule

The creation discipline of beginning with the smallest set of seed facts that force the world to become itself, then expanding only along pressure lines.

## Tonal inevitability

The sense that an event follows from the world’s genre, mood, motif, and moral or symbolic grammar even when it is not realist causation.

## Traceability

The ability to show which doctrine, protocol, checklist, template, or decision record supports a fact, instrument, repair, or branch result.

## Transaction cost

The cost of finding, negotiating, proving, enforcing, trusting, coordinating, monitoring, or exiting an exchange. In worldbuilding, transaction costs explain why markets, guilds, brokers, courts, kin networks, oaths, black markets, and monopolies form.

## Truth layer

A classification of where a claim sits relative to canon and in-world belief: objective canon, author-secret canon, branch canon, mystery boundary, diegetic claim, public belief, local belief, elite belief, mythic truth, propaganda, lie, honest error, disputed claim.

## Wonder / awe / sublimity

A non-instrumental effect of magnitude, beauty, terror, enchantment, strangeness, or vastness that matters because it changes attention, humility, fear, reverence, or desire, not because it hides a puzzle answer.

## World kernel

The smallest set of premises, commitments, consequence modes, constraints, tone, protected effects, and seed facts that define what world is being built.

## World-state health

The degree to which a world’s facts have prerequisites, constraints, consequences appropriate to mode, institutions or mode-equivalent pressure, contradictions, mysteries, protected wonder/sacredness/horror/symbolic excess, uncertainty, costs, latencies, path dependence, and residue in healthy relation.
