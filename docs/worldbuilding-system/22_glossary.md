# 22. Glossary

## Action arena

A recurring situation where actors interact under rules, incentives, information limits, resources, and consequences.

## Actor

An entity capable of intention or meaningful action: person, group, institution, species, spirit, AI-like mind, deity, faction, household, corporation, cult, or swarm.

## Aesthetic residue

Visible, audible, linguistic, symbolic, material, or tonal evidence that a fact has shaped the world.

## Branch canon

A continuity where some facts diverge from another continuity while remaining governed rather than discarded.

## Canon

The set of facts treated as authoritative for a given continuity, branch, scope, and purpose.

## Canon fact

A fact the world must answer to.

## Canon status

The current standing of a claim in canon governance: proposed, under review, accepted, accepted with constraints, localized, contested, quarantined, branch-only, superseded, deprecated, or rejected.

## Capability

A repeatable ability, method, technology, power, ritual, skill, biological trait, or institutional permission that lets actors do something.

## Claim

A statement made by a source or actor. A claim may be true, false, mistaken, propaganda, rumor, myth, branch-specific, or unknown.

## Constraint

A limit, bottleneck, cost, scope boundary, failure mode, taboo, rule, access condition, or dependency that shapes how a fact can operate.

## Constraint tag

A concise label describing how an accepted fact is limited: local, rare, recent, secret, misunderstood, costly, unstable, seasonal, jurisdictional, class-bound, model-limited, ritual-bound, branch-bound, and so on.

## Consequence

A change the world undergoes because a fact is true.

## Counter-institution

An unofficial, rival, underground, illegal, informal, rebel, sacred, mutual-aid, or black-market structure created in response to official failure or suppression.

## Decision operation

A governance action applied to a proposal: accept, constrain, localize, historicize, reinterpret, institutionalize, price, branch, quarantine, supersede, deprecate, or reject.

## Diegetic claim

A claim made inside the world by a character, institution, text, tradition, rumor, or artifact.

## Domain

One of the world areas through which facts propagate: metaphysics, physics, ecology, geography, technology, magic, biology, psychology, society, politics, economy, history, language, aesthetics.

## Evidence habitat

The social and material setting that lets a belief persist: source, institution, ritual, incentive, memory, punishment, identity, or convenience.

## Fact admission

The process of deciding whether and how a proposed fact enters canon.

## Fact type

A practical classification used during admission: existence, capability, constraint, event, history, institution, relationship, resource, rule, exception, belief, mystery, aesthetic, branch.

## Fossil

Residue from an older fact or era: ruin, name, law, ritual, tool, border, scar, taboo, proverb, costume, office, inequality, or myth.

## Governance

Human stewardship of canon: proposing, reviewing, accepting, constraining, branching, quarantining, repairing, retiring, and recording.

## Institution

A stable pattern of rules-in-use, norms, roles, rights, obligations, sanctions, procedures, expectations, and enforcement. Institutions may be formal or informal.

## Localized fact

A fact accepted only within a defined place, group, route, jurisdiction, institution, species, era, class, ritual, or branch.

## Mystery boundary

The line protecting what is fixed, unknown, unknowable, author-secret, disputed, or deliberately undecided.

## Objective canon

What is true in the active continuity regardless of what characters believe.

## Propagation

The process by which facts create consequences across domains, scales, times, places, actors, institutions, and aesthetics.

## Public belief

What a broad population believes, whether or not it is objectively true.

## Quarantine

A status for a proposal or fact that must not be used as active canon until its contradictions or implications are resolved.

## Repair operation

A method for resolving contradiction or instability: clarify scope, add constraint, price, localize, historicize, institutionalize, diffuse unevenly, reinterpret, split, retcon, quarantine, reject.

## Retcon

A change in how earlier canon is interpreted or replaced.

## Shock cone

The widening pattern of consequences produced when a fact enters the world: immediate effects, domain ripples, institutional adaptation, second-order adaptations, fossils, countermeasures, and distant echoes.

## Source

A person, institution, artifact, tradition, record, sensor, ritual, memory, document, witness, or performance that supports a claim.

## Status

See canon status.

## Suppression

The deliberate distortion, concealment, criminalization, monopolization, erasure, or weaponization of a fact or evidence.

## Truth layer

A classification of where a claim sits relative to canon and in-world belief: objective canon, author-secret canon, branch canon, mystery boundary, diegetic claim, public belief, local belief, elite belief, mythic truth, propaganda, lie, honest error, disputed claim.

## World kernel

The smallest set of premises, commitments, constraints, tone, and seed facts that define what world is being built.

## World-state health

The degree to which a world’s facts have prerequisites, constraints, consequences, institutions, contradictions, mysteries, and residue in healthy relation.
