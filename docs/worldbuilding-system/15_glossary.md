# 15. Glossary

## Action arena

A recurring situation where actors make choices under rules, incentives, information, and constraints.

## Actor

Any entity capable of goals, strategies, choices, or adaptation.

## Aesthetic contradiction

A fact that may technically fit but damages the world’s intended identity or feeling.

## Branch canon

A fact true only in one possible continuity.

## Canon fact

A statement accepted into the authorial world model.

## Capability

A repeatable ability to produce an effect.

## Change package

The full set of consequences, constraints, repairs, and records required to admit a fact.

## Constraint

A limit that prevents a capability or fact from transforming too much too cheaply.

## Continuity

Consistency of stated facts, names, dates, events, and rules.

## Causal coherence

The degree to which facts produce plausible consequences across the world.

## Daily-life residue

Ordinary habits, tools, phrases, fears, prices, and routines that show a fact has social reality.

## Diegetic claim

A statement made within the fictional world.

## Diffusion

The spread of knowledge, capability, custom, disease, technology, magic, or belief through a world.

## Externality

A cost or benefit imposed on people who did not choose the action causing it.

## Fact admission

The process by which proposed canon becomes accepted, constrained, branched, quarantined, or rejected.

## Frontloaded seed

A fact introduced in the initial world premise.

## Historical residue

Wars, ruins, laws, holidays, trauma, borders, institutions, and memory left behind by past facts.

## Institution

A durable social pattern that coordinates behavior, including formal organizations and informal customs.

## Mystery boundary

The defined edge between what may be known and what must remain unknown.

## Objective canon

True in the authorial model.

## Path dependence

The way earlier events and institutions shape later possibilities.

## Propagation

The process of tracing consequences through domains of the world.

## Rule-in-use

The rule people actually follow, as distinct from the official rule.

## Scarcity

A limited resource or condition that shapes behavior.

## Shock cone

The expanding orders of consequence produced by a new fact.

## Soft contradiction

A tension that can be repaired with explanation or constraint.

## Truth layer

The status of a statement relative to authorial truth, public belief, myth, error, propaganda, mystery, or branch continuity.
