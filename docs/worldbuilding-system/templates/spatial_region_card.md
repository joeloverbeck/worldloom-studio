# Spatial Region Card

Use for a place, route, borderland, biome, city, valley, sea, district, ruin field, sacred zone, or diffusion region.

## Region

## Physical constraints

Terrain, climate, water, soil, minerals, disease, season, hazards.

## Settlement pattern

Cities, villages, nomads, ruins, roads, ports, forts, farms, camps, pilgrimage sites.

## Routes and barriers

How do people, goods, rumors, armies, disease, magic, and styles move?

## Political geography

Borders, jurisdictions, claims, patrols, contested zones, maps, tolls.

## Ecological relations

Species, crops, predators, pests, sacred animals, invasive forces.

## Institutions in place

Local rules, offices, markets, shrines, guilds, kin systems, informal practices.

## Regional variation of key facts

What is true here but not elsewhere?

## Diffusion status

Origin / early adoption / saturated / resisted / bypassed / suppressed / fossilized.

## Aesthetic residue

Materials, colors, buildings, clothing, accents, food, sounds, rituals, place names.

## Negative space

What expected consequence is absent here, and why?

## Traceability note
