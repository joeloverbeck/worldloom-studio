## Structural summary of iteration four

Iteration 4 keeps the iteration-3 file inventory: 24 numbered documents (`00`-`23`), package `README.md`, `manifest.json`, 12 checklists, and 18 templates. No files were deleted, renamed, split, merged, or added inside the package.

This was an intentional no-downgrade decision. The audit found that the iteration-3 architecture already had a coherent authority tier and every original file remained load-bearing. File-level churn would have created more risk than value because checklists and templates were already wired to parent protocols. The improvement therefore happens inside the files:

- stronger authority-order and traceability language in `README.md` and `21_templates_index.md`;
- a clean iteration-4 provenance record replacing the stale iteration-2 baseline in the old `00`;
- sharper decision-jurisdiction separation among canon status, constraint tag, admission operation, and repair operation;
- new canon-debt vocabulary for accepted-but-unfinished work;
- stronger anti-over-simulation rules so the method remains usable;
- deeper spatial diffusion, institutional/economic, bounded-agency, uncertainty, transmedia, semiotic, QA, and AI-oversight guidance;
- expanded checklist and template completion rules while preserving their human-instrument framing.

## Research rationale

Iteration 4 used external research only as external evidence, not as target-repository evidence.

Systems thinking remains useful because Meadows's leverage-point framing supports looking for high-pressure places where small changes alter incentives, rules, information, goals, and feedback rather than exhaustively listing trivia.[^meadows]

Spatial protocol updates draw on Rogers's diffusion framing: innovations spread through innovation properties, communication channels, time, and social systems, so geography must be paired with trust, legitimacy, network, and adoption channels.[^rogers]

Institutional/economic/suppression updates draw on North's institution-as-constraint framing, Ostrom's action-arena and rules-in-use language, Williamson's transaction-cost economics, and Scott's warning that administrative legibility simplifies and distorts local realities.[^north][^ostrom][^williamson][^scott]

Agent updates preserve the package's bounded-agency law and sharpen it with Simon's bounded rationality: actors do not need omniscient optimization; they need situated, locally intelligible decision pressure.[^simon]

Uncertainty updates draw on Bayesian epistemology and belief revision as craft analogies: confidence strength, evidence, prior belief, and belief-updating behavior matter, even when no formal probability calculus is used.[^bayes][^beliefrevision]

Transmedia and game updates draw on Jenkins's transmedia principles, Klastrup and Tosca's mythos/topos/ethos model of transmedial worlds, and Hunicke/LeBlanc/Zubek's MDA distinction between mechanics, dynamics, and aesthetics.[^jenkins][^klastrup][^mda]

Aesthetic updates draw on Peirce's icon/index/symbol distinction and Silverstein's indexicality work to keep style tied to social meaning and world evidence rather than decorative clutter.[^peirce][^silverstein]

AI-workflow updates draw on NIST and OECD AI governance materials to reinforce that AI can propose or audit but human stewards remain accountable for canon decisions.[^nist][^oecd]
