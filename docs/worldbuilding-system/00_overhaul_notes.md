## Structural summary of iteration five

No files were added, renamed, split, merged, or deleted. The package remains 56 files: 24 numbered documents (`00`–`23`), `README.md`, `manifest.json`, 12 checklists, and 18 templates.

That was a deliberate no-downgrade decision. The priority problems did not require layout churn. The clearest repair was to strengthen doctrine, protocols, QA, examples, and instruments in place while keeping every existing file identity and parent-protocol relationship intact.

## Priority-resolution summary

### Priority A — mode-aware consequence for non-naturalistic worlds

Resolved by making consequence mode load-bearing rather than a caveat. `01_core_theory.md` now names material/institutional, mythic/ritual, fairy-tale/folktale, dream/surreal, comic/absurd, theological/sacred, horror/dread, and lyrical/symbolic consequence modes. Institutional and economic rigor remain hard gates for realist worlds, but non-naturalistic worlds can now score strongly through recurrence, ritual logic, symbolic transformation, tonal inevitability, sacred opacity, dread, comic escalation, and ordinary-life residue.

The change propagates through creation, admission, propagation, constraints, temporal/spatial protocols, agent psychology, institutional analysis, extraction, QA, checklists, templates, and glossary. `19_worked_examples.md` adds a fully worked fairy-tale/lyrical example, “The village where broken promises become birds,” to demonstrate consequence without institutional naturalism.

### Priority B — mystery distinguished from wonder, sacredness, horror, and symbolic excess

Resolved by keeping the old mystery machinery and adding a preservation layer. `13_contradiction_retcon_and_mystery.md` now distinguishes governed mystery from wonder/awe/sublimity, sacred opacity, horror/terror/dread, and symbolic excess. `17_aesthetic_coherence_and_semiosis.md` explains how those effects operate as aesthetic consequence. `checklists/mystery_preservation.md` and `templates/mystery_ledger_entry.md` now allow a puzzle question of `none` so a protected effect is not forced into a hidden-answer frame.

### Priority C — calibrated QA exemplar anchors

Resolved in `18_quality_assurance_tests.md`. The 0–3 scale and existing interpretation bands remain. A new mode-aware scoring note and weak/adequate/strong exemplar anchors for all 30 core tests give stewards comparison points without pretending the rubric has mathematical precision.

## Research rationale

Version `0.5` adds research support in three focused lanes.

For non-naturalistic worlds, Propp's morphology supports the idea that folktales can be coherent through recurrent functions, motifs, positions, prohibitions, helpers, tests, and transformations rather than through realist sociology. This grounds the new recurrence and symbolic-transformation tests.

For wonder and protected opacity, Burke, Kant, Otto, Bennett, Freud, Carroll, Windsor, Peirce, and Ricoeur support distinctions among sublimity, aesthetic judgment, numinous sacredness, enchantment, uncanny horror, dread, sign types, and symbol as surplus meaning. These sources shaped the new separation among mystery, wonder, sacred opacity, horror/dread, and symbolic excess.

For QA calibration, research on calibrated exemplars and rubrics supports using weak/adequate/strong sample answers as rating anchors. Version `0.5` does not claim empirical inter-rater reliability; it gives stewards better comparison points and leaves full validation as future work.

See `23_research_notes_and_bibliography.md` for full source notes.
