# Contradiction, Retcon, and Mystery Flow Spec

This spec defines Worldloom Studio's fourth guided flow: `13_contradiction_retcon_and_mystery.md`, `templates/contradiction_report.md`, `templates/mystery_ledger_entry.md`, and `checklists/mystery_preservation.md`. It is downstream of `docs/specs/schema-v1.md`, `docs/specs/admission-flow.md`, `docs/specs/propagation-flow.md`, `docs/worldbuilding-system/13_contradiction_retcon_and_mystery.md`, `docs/worldbuilding-system/templates/contradiction_report.md`, `docs/worldbuilding-system/templates/mystery_ledger_entry.md`, `docs/worldbuilding-system/checklists/mystery_preservation.md`, `docs/worldbuilding-system/20_ai_assisted_workflow.md`, the foundational principles, and ADRs 0001-0004.

The flow governs contradictions, repairs, retcons, and protected mysteries after propagation has exposed pressure. It never detects contradictions for the steward, never admits new facts by a repair side door, and never lets mystery language erase consequences.

## Fixed Decisions

1. **Corrective vocabulary migration.** A forward-only migration reseeds `repair_operation` to the twelve operations in `13` and `templates/contradiction_report.md`: `clarify scope`, `add constraint`, `price the fact`, `localize`, `historicize`, `institutionalize`, `diffuse unevenly`, `reinterpret`, `split`, `retcon`, `quarantine`, and `reject`. The outcome terms `branch`, `supersede`, and `deprecate` leave the repair vocabulary because the template says to record branch, supersession, and deprecation separately as resulting status or branch decisions.
2. **New controlled vocabularies.** Four vocabularies are added with package citations. `contradiction_disposition` uses `13`: `not a contradiction`, `diegetic conflict`, `mystery-preserving conflict`, `repair required`, `branch required`, `deprecation required`, and `rejection required`. `preservation_operation` uses `13` and `checklists/mystery_preservation.md`: `reveal`, `delay`, `forbid`, `consecrate`, `dread-preserve`, `excess-preserve`, and `translate`. `retcon_type` uses `13`: `soft retcon`, `hard retcon`, `diegetic retcon`, `branch retcon`, `audience-facing retcon`, and `back-office retcon`. `protected_effect_type` uses `13` and `templates/mystery_ledger_entry.md`: `governed mystery`, `wonder-awe-sublimity`, `sacred opacity`, `horror-terror-dread`, and `symbolic excess`; `hybrid` is seeded as an extensible package-template term.
3. **Known divergence log.** `templates/contradiction_report.md` says work scale can be `foundational`. The app keeps `work_scale` aligned to `06_canon_fact_admission_protocol.md` (`minor`, `moderate`, `major`, `severe`, `catastrophic`) and records the template wording as a known seed divergence rather than amending the package.
4. **Entry surfaces.** A contradiction run starts either from any existing record, linking implicated records into the report, or as a free-standing report for prose or many-record conflicts. No app-side contradiction detection is introduced; the steward notices, the app clerks.
5. **Run persistence.** Runs use existing `flow_instances` and are resumable and interleavable. The run writes one append-only `contradiction_report` as the master audit record.
6. **Triage composition.** Triage stores sectioned prose for the package steps: incompatible claims, truth layers, affected scope, who can notice, audience notice, contradiction type, higher-authority material, and mystery/protected-effect relationship. Doctrine excerpts from `13` are rendered at point of use as versioned derivations.
7. **Severity declaration.** Work scale is steward-declared with vocabulary definitions at point of use. There is no default, suggestion, or inference.
8. **Disposition gate.** A report cannot close without one `contradiction_disposition`. The app checks coverage and required substance only; the steward owns the judgment.
9. **Repair as second governed authority.** A repair-required, deprecation-required, or rejection-required disposition may change existing record standing under repair jurisdiction. Repair operations are ordered, primary first, and drawn only from `repair_operation`.
10. **Jurisdiction symmetry.** Admission paths refuse repair operations and repair paths refuse admission decision operations. Repair operation records are separate from resulting canon status or branch decisions.
11. **Record lifecycle for repairs.** Repaired card-regime records update in place: repaired wording becomes present tense, outgoing wording moves to history, and the contradiction report remains unedited.
12. **Repair-created facts.** Any new fact surfaced by a repair routes through the universal admission queue at `proposed`, linked `derived_from` the contradiction report. Repairs repair; only admission admits.
13. **Retcon substance.** Any repair composition including `retcon` requires one `retcon_type` and at least one named retcon cost from `13`'s ledger: continuity, institutional, character, mystery, aesthetic, or future. The retcon kindness rule is shown at point of use.
14. **Repair propagation.** Close offers repair propagation. If assigned, it mints open `canon_debt` scoped to propagation and links it from the contradiction report as follow-up. If declined, a `skip_record` is written; reason text is required only at major-or-higher work scale.
15. **Owed-boundaries queue.** The queue is a view over propagation disposition rows marked `protected as a mystery boundary` that lack a linked `mystery_ledger_entry`. It is not a table. Working an item creates or updates a ledger entry and links it `preserves_boundary_for` the protected record and `derived_from` the propagation report that minted the boundary.
16. **Mystery ledger entries.** Ledger entries are card-regime `mystery_ledger_entry` records with sectioned prose for the protected-effect minimum: protected effect type, puzzle question or `none`, what is fixed, what is secret or undecided, damaging explanations, preserved consequences, reveal permissions, reveal prohibitions, recurrence or motif, and what would break if solved or flattened. The facets `protected_effect_type`, `mystery_state`, and `preservation_boundary` are steward-chosen.
17. **Mystery-preservation checklist.** A repair, reveal, or retcon touching a protected unknown offers the checklist and records a chosen `preservation_operation`. The sacred-opacity accountability guard renders when the protected effect is sacred opacity or adjacent sacred/horror opacity. Declines write skip records.
18. **Mystery-preserving conflict gate.** A `mystery-preserving conflict` disposition cannot close without a completed preservation checklist run. Mystery cannot silently excuse an ordinary repair.
19. **Sovereignty loop.** Two default prompt templates are added as versioned derivations of `20_ai_assisted_workflow.md`: `repair_challenge` for pressure on quoted claims, chosen repair, and costs; `boundary_guard` for pressure on preservation boundaries and explanation-pressure choices. Prompt-out is offered at dependency-bearing steps and never required.
20. **Advisory artifacts and skips.** Pasted responses remain immutable `advisory_artifact` records with standing rulings. Informed mutations link advisory sources. Every declined offered instrument writes a `skip_record`; major-or-higher skips require reasons, below-threshold skips record that no reason was collected.
21. **Provenance.** Every repair mutation records actor, timestamp, flow step, and advisory derivation where applicable through existing provenance, link, and jurisdiction-event substrates.

## Testing Seams

Primary seam: the localhost HTTP API against temp-file world databases for vocabulary listing, contradiction report entry from a record and free-standing, run resume, triage capture, steward-declared scale, close refusal, disposition close, full repair, repair-created admission proposal, retcon cost, propagation debt, owed-boundaries queue, mystery ledger, preservation checklist, prompt generation, advisory paste/disposition, informed mutation links, and skip thresholds.

Secondary seam: direct store/SQL assertions for forward migration with pre-migration backup, corrective vocabulary reseed, newer-schema rejection, append-only contradiction reports, close refusal rules, jurisdiction symmetry, repair status transitions, card history, advisory immutability, owed-boundary link integrity, and prompt-template versioning.

No new testing seams are introduced.

## Principles

Touches `charter.md` (P-3, P-4, T-8), `canon-sovereignty.md` (P-2, W-1, T-5), `domain-fidelity.md` (P-1, T-2), `workflow-principles.md` (P-5, W-2, W-3, W-4, W-7), `data-principles.md` (P-6, W-5, W-6, T-3, T-4, T-5), and ADRs 0001-0004. It affirms non-contradiction: the app clerks flow state, queues, gates, links, provenance, and vocabulary jurisdiction while the steward supplies claims, classifications, repairs, costs, boundaries, and final wording.

T-8 coverage statement: `13` and its contradiction/mystery instruments are the next field-tested spine surface after propagation. Uncertainty, belief, evidence, QA, specialized pass flows, branch mechanics, app-side contradiction detection, Markdown export, LLM API integration, and multi-steward features stay out of this slice.
