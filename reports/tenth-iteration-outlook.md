# Tenth-Iteration Outlook — Causal Canon Worldbuilding System

*Written 2026-07-02 by the session that performed the version-1.0 release pass (Claude Code, Fable model, working from HEAD `b2580da`, leaving the version-1.0 working tree uncommitted). Because the author is also the executor — and because this pass shipped the number everyone wanted to ship — this outlook is written adversarially against its own release: the question is not "was the gate met" (the trial answered that) but "what does the 1.0 label now put at risk, and what would a hostile reviewer say we are still pretending not to know."*

## 1. Warnings and weaknesses of version 1.0 as released

### 1.1 The label will be read as broader than the claim

The coverage statement is honest, but honesty in a subsection does not govern how a version number travels. "1.0" will be repeated in places the coverage statement is not — a commit message, a project README, a conversation — and every such repetition silently drops the qualifier. The concrete exposure:

- **Five protocols have never been run in anger:** `10` (spatial), `11` (agent/psychology), `14` (uncertainty/evidence), `16` (extraction), `17` (aesthetic coherence). Both trials' QA profiles put their 2-scores exactly there, which means both trial worlds are *carrying named debt that only these untested protocols can resolve*. The first person to run `10` on Carillon's hinterland or `14` on its belief ecology is doing what both trial stewards deferred — on a version number that no longer signals "expect rough edges."
- **The entire multi-steward tier (`15`, workflow roles, dispute handling, decision records) has zero field exposure.** This is a different failure surface — contested authority, not solo confusion — and no amount of solo-trial convergence transfers to it. A team adopting "a 1.0 methodology" for collaborative canon work is running an untested instrument at its most political seam.
- **`19`/`20` under a genuinely naive steward.** Both trial stewards were methodology-literate and had read the prior trial's log. The on-ramp's measured success (zero off-path reads, ~4,500-line core path) is a success for *prepared* cold entry. The package has never met the user it will most often get: someone who skims, starts in the middle, and reaches `19` only when already confused.
- **The aesthetic-promise disposal outside institutional/material-primary worlds.** Both trial worlds were institutional-primary; in both, aesthetic promises were secondary cargo. In a lyrical- or sacred-primary world they are the load-bearing facts, and the disposal ("admit as kernel commitments, check at QA, card what turns load-bearing") may be structurally under-built there. This is the single most likely place a future trial produces a *structural* finding — i.e., the most credible threat to the convergence story.

None of this argues against the 1.0 call — the gate the last two outlooks defined was met, cleanly. It argues that the coverage statement is now the package's most load-bearing paragraph, and any future edit that shortens or softens it is a downgrade disguised as tidying.

### 1.2 Tensions the five fixes themselves created

Each fix was demanded by the trial log, but sentences have shadows:

- **F2-01 (pass-generated facts route through admission).** The rule is jurisdictionally correct and the trial steward followed it by inference before it existed. But stated baldly in three protocols, it slightly raises the ceremony floor of every specialized pass: a steward whose temporal pass surfaces six small era facts now reads an instruction to gate them. The ledger-row escape valve is named in `07`'s sentence, and severity scaling still governs — but if a future trial reports pass-time admission fatigue, the repair is to point harder at cluster-gating (the trial gated addendum batches as single clusters, correctly), not to weaken the route.
- **F2-02 (scorecard of record is per-test).** Declaring the nine-row worksheet a triage view makes the 28-row expansion normative for a full QA pass. That is what both trials actually did, so the fix codifies practice — but it also makes the *lightest* legitimate QA pass heavier on paper than 0.8 implied. The mitigation is already in `18` ("do not chase all 3s"; n/a-with-reason), but a hostile reviewer would note the package's QA cost now rounds up.
- **Q2-02 (decision record owed at catastrophic band only).** This clause *reduces* record-keeping at the major/foundational repair tier for solo stewards — correct per the work scale, and exactly what the trial steward derived. The shadow: a solo world that later gains collaborators will have Level-5 repairs on file with no decision records, and the new contributors inherit contradiction reports as the only reconstruction of *why*. The contradiction report's mandatory sections carry most of that weight, which is why the clause is safe — but the clause is the first place in `06` that tells a steward they owe *less* than they feared, and such sentences get quoted out of scale.
- **Q2-01 (the pass report container).** Sanctioned deliberately as a container with no template, no completion rule of its own, and no glossary entry — exceeding the finding would have meant a new instrument family. The cost of that restraint: the container's minimum contents are defined only by the sweeps' completion rules, and a steward who wants to know "what must a pass report contain?" must compose the answer from `21`'s sentence plus the sweep. If a future trial logs that composition as a friction, the answer is one sentence more in `21`, still not a template.
- **The operating card grew again.** Two re-syncs this pass (path step 8, severity-map row). Each was owed — the card must track its parents — but the card's value is that it is one page, and it has gained lines in two consecutive iterations. The drift liability the ninth outlook named is now compounded by a creep liability: the card's next steward should be more willing to *compress* than to append.

### 1.3 The standing epistemic caveat: two trials, one model family

Both field trials were run by stewards of the same model family as the package's authors, and both disclosed reading the prior trial's log for methodology. The convergence evidence is therefore *internally* consistent in a way that flatters the package: stewards who think like the authors find the authors' instruments natural. The three highest-information future trials (below) are ranked precisely because each breaks one axis of this contamination — mode (sacred/lyrical-primary), preparation (naive steward), and authority structure (real collaborators). Until at least one of them runs, "two trials converged" should be quoted with its footnote.

### 1.4 Standing agnosticism risks (carried forward, updated)

- **`manifest.json` as proto-schema.** Still grandfathered software-shaped inventory; this pass kept it flat. Resist every future temptation to add fields the methodology does not need — the file is an inventory, not an API.
- **Operating-card productization.** The hosting repository is a web app, and the card is the file most shaped like an onboarding screen. If the app productizes it, keep the methodology card and the product UI as separate artifacts with separate change control; the card's authority chain (parents govern) must not acquire a software dependent.
- **The twin Saltmarrows.** The package's `19` example and the frozen trial artifacts in the repository's reports area continue to diverge by design (this pass added a V2-20 line to the package copy only). Do not "sync" them; the package copy is canonical for the package, the trial artifacts are immutable evidence. The same now applies to Carillon: nothing in the package cites it, and nothing should.
- **This pass introduced no new coupling** — verified: no package file cites a repository path outside the package; the coverage statement names protocol surfaces only.

### 1.5 A release-pass-specific warning

This outlook is written by the session that performed the release, hours after the trial log recommended it. The two consecutive "declare 1.0" signals (trial recommendation 2, user acceptance) were independent of this session, and the substantive work was five sentences whose shapes the trial itself proposed — so the room for self-grade inflation is small but not zero. It is concentrated in two places a reader can check directly: whether the finding→disposition table in `00_overhaul_notes.md` really covers every ledger item (it is enumerable), and whether the coverage statement's honestly-untested list really matches the trial log's own list (it is quotable). Both checks take minutes; a skeptical reader should run them rather than trust this paragraph.

## 2. Closing convergence re-score

The four criteria originate in the eighth-iteration outlook §2, were re-scored by the ninth-iteration outlook §2, and are scored here for the last time against the second trial plus this pass — closing the lineage.

1. **A full iteration passes with doctrine (`01`–`04`) untouched.** **Met.** The ninth iteration is complete — trial plus repair — and `01`–`04` are byte-identical to 0.8, verified by diff. The one criterion the ninth outlook scored "not met, by a deliberate margin" is now satisfied exactly as it predicted a sentence-fix pass would satisfy it.
2. **At least one end-to-end field trial whose friction log produces only minor edits.** **Met, twice over, for the exercised paths.** Trial 1's log produced a sentences-plus-two-small-files pass; trial 2's log produced five sentences. The scope caveat stands permanently: this criterion has never been met for `10`, `11`, `14`–`17`, or any collaborative path, and the coverage statement now says so in the package itself.
3. **The severity question settled by use.** **Met, and re-confirmed under stress.** Trial 2 stressed the two-scale mapping harder than trial 1 (a promotion, two composed repairs, a Level-5 chain) and produced only two marginal wobbles — a level-classification parenthetical (F2-03) and a decision-record derivation (Q2-02) — both fixed with sentences that *read* the existing scales rather than amending them. The two-vocabulary design is settled doctrine absent field failure.
4. **An on-ramp shaped by observed need.** **Met in design and now in use.** The half of this criterion the ninth outlook left open ("the shape has not been [proven]") closed in trial 2: cold entry through the routed path with zero forced off-path reads, and the operating card open beside every stage without a drift finding.

**The gate:** the ninth-iteration outlook defined 1.0-ripeness as *a second field trial, run cold on 0.8, whose friction log demands no structural change and confirms the 0.8 additions carry their weight.* Both clauses were satisfied by the trial and the release honored its terms: no structural change was made because none was demanded. **The gate was satisfied at release.** Version 1.0 is, on the stated evidence and within the stated coverage, an honest number.

## 3. Verdict: is a tenth iteration warranted?

**As a document-editing pass: no — and this is now a three-outlook consensus with two trials of evidence behind it.** Iteration 7's outlook argued editing had hit diminishing returns; iteration 8 consumed the first trial's backlog; iteration 9 consumed the second's, which was five sentences. A tenth editing pass would have nothing to spend but self-derived diagnosis — the retired failure mode. The package should not be edited again until a field log demands it. This includes well-intentioned "polish": with 1.0 declared, every unforced edit is now a risk to a certified surface, and the no-downgrade set (both trials' validated behaviors, enumerated in `00_overhaul_notes.md`) is the acceptance test any future wording change must pass.

**As future field work: yes, when wanted — in the trial's own priority order, adopted here with reasons:**

1. **A sacred- or lyrical-primary world.** Highest information per unit of effort: it tests the aesthetic-promise disposal and the mode-equivalent machinery (`17`, the mode-aware QA calibration, the preservation instruments) where they are load-bearing rather than secondary — the one place §1.1 rates a *structural* finding credible. If the package survives that trial with sentence-level friction, its equal-weight non-naturalistic claim is earned in the field rather than asserted.
2. **A genuinely naive-steward trial.** Someone with no trial-log exposure and no methodology stake, ideally human. Tests `19` routing, `20`, and the on-ramp uncontaminated — and breaks the model-family loop of §1.3.
3. **The collaboration trial — when, and only when, a second real contributor exists.** The last untested tier (`15`, roles, disputes, decision records). Deferred, not dismissed; it is a poor use of a solo budget and becomes urgent the day it stops being hypothetical.

Each trial, if run, generates its own evidence-bounded repair pass *only if its log demands one* — the pattern iterations 8 and 9 have now proven twice. There is no schedule; the package is complete as released, and further versions should exist only as consequences of use.

**The adversarial bottom line.** The satisfaction of shipping 1.0 is exactly the bias this verdict must resist, so state the falsifiers plainly: 1.0's convergence story dies if a mode-primary trial finds the aesthetic disposal structurally under-built, if a naive steward cannot enter through the on-ramp without off-path reads, or if the first collaboration exposes the governance tier as armchair design. All three are live possibilities the coverage statement deliberately leaves open. What two trials have actually established is narrower and still valuable: for a solo, methodology-literate steward building an institutional-primary world, the package works end-to-end, its instruments generate design value rather than paperwork, and its doctrine has stopped moving. That is what "1.0" means here — no more, and honestly no less.
