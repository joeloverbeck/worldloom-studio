## Recommended First PRD

### Redeemable Conditional-Pass Deferral

Purpose: let the steward resume or complete a previously deferred specialized pass without deleting its governed history or leaving the workflow map permanently wrong.

Sources: Field Build 19 `F-01` and App Seed 3; the preserved field-world readback; closed #372/#375/#377; current Conditional-pass persistence/routes/tests and workflow-map browser surface; methodology `03` and `07`; workflow-map, schema, and browser-visible guidance specs; P-5, P-2, W-4/W-5/W-6/W-7/W-8/W-9/W-10, T-3/T-5; and ADRs 0008-0009.

Problem: deferral is a one-way transition. Once an obligation is deferred, its source-selected route disappears, no reinstate action exists, and matching completed-pass evidence cannot cover it. The map may then say the handoff is fully governed even after the steward has resumed and completed the real work elsewhere.

Recommended product rule or seam:

1. Keep the current obligation dispositions `outstanding`, `deferred`, and `covered`; add a governed `reinstated` transition event from deferred back to outstanding rather than adding a fourth current disposition.
2. Reinstatement requires an explicit steward action and non-empty reason. It clears the current deferral rationale only from the current-state projection while preserving every deferral rationale and transition in immutable governed history.
3. A matching completed pass may cover either an outstanding or deferred obligation. Deferred-to-covered coverage records `deferred` as the prior state, keeps the deferral event visible, and records the completed report as current coverage evidence.
4. Covered obligations are terminal for this lifecycle. They cannot be deferred or reinstated; later correction belongs to the specialized pass/report lifecycle, not the obligation ledger.
5. Reinstated obligations become routeable and owed again. A fresh workflow-map read recomputes outstanding/governed counts, stable ordering, primary next decision, and current/next/resume state from the server-owned current disposition while keeping Admission visible and navigable.
6. Repeated valid defer/reinstate cycles preserve each transition as a distinct event with actor, timestamp, reason, flow step, prior state, and resulting state. Request retries remain idempotent without suppressing a later genuinely new cycle.
7. Invalid source/report/pass identity, empty reason, stale transition state, incompatible repeats, and ambiguous matching coverage are refused atomically with exact blockers and no sibling or unrelated mutation.
8. The browser renders the server-returned `Reinstate obligation` action, proposed write, untouched state, required reason, failure recovery, current state, and complete history. It does not infer transition legality.
9. Existing worlds with deferred obligations gain the action through forward migration without rewriting prior obligation rows, deferral events, source facts, Propagation reports, completed pass reports, Admission contents, canon debt, or sibling obligations.

Scope:

- Workflow-map, schema, and browser-evidence contracts for reinstatement and deferred coverage.
- Conditional-pass-owned persistence and HTTP actions for reinstate, direct deferred-to-covered matching, repeat transition history, atomicity, and readback.
- Workflow-map response state, action previews, counts, ordering, orientation, and read-side trail.
- Browser controls for reinstatement reason, adjacent failure recovery, route restoration, history, and accessible state changes.
- Focused tests, historical-world migration proof, one production workflow-map walkthrough, and replay of the original deferral boundary.

Acceptance:

1. Deferring one outstanding obligation with a rationale preserves the current #375 behavior, leaves siblings unchanged, and makes the handoff governed.
2. A deferred obligation exposes a server-owned reinstate action with proposed write, untouched-state list, and required reason; successful reinstatement changes only that obligation to outstanding and appends a `deferred → outstanding` event.
3. After reinstatement, `Follow source-selected pass` is available again, the correct pass/source/report input is preserved, outstanding/governed counts refresh, and the obligation returns to its stable ordered place in the primary decision.
4. A matching completed pass can move a deferred obligation directly to covered without prior reinstatement; history retains the deferral and records `deferred → covered` with the covering report.
5. Completing a mismatched, incomplete, wrong-source, wrong-report, or wrong-pass run cannot cover the deferred obligation.
6. Covered obligations refuse defer and reinstate actions without partial mutation.
7. At least two defer/reinstate cycles preserve distinct reasons, timestamps, actors, flow steps, and state transitions; retrying one submitted action creates no duplicate event.
8. Empty reason, stale current state, incompatible repeat, and forced persistence failure return exact errors and remediation while preserving the authoritative row, draft reason, sibling rows, source/report records, and Admission state.
9. Existing deferred rows migrate without changing their current state or historical event content, then support reinstatement and direct matching coverage.
10. The workflow-map browser visibly distinguishes outstanding, deferred, reinstated-current-as-outstanding, and covered history; announces transition success/failure; retains keyboard focus and entered reason on failure; and shows current, next, resume, and safe return state.
11. A docs-naive production walkthrough defers one obligation, returns to the map, reinstates it, follows the source-selected pass, and confirms current routing and history; a second scenario completes matching work while deferred and confirms automatic coverage.
12. Root gates `pnpm test`, `pnpm typecheck`, and `pnpm build` pass on the final implementation tree.

Likely issue slices:

1. Conditional-pass redemption spec, schema transition contract, and browser-evidence mapping.
2. Server reinstatement, repeat history, deferred coverage, migration, and workflow-map recomputation.
3. Browser reinstatement controls, failure recovery, route restoration, and history.
4. Historical-world and active-route replay with preservation and closeout evidence.

Out of scope:

- Reopening or modifying closed Temporal Prompt-out or revision work from #379/#380.
- Deleting, overwriting, or hiding prior deferral events.
- Reopening a covered obligation or correcting a completed specialized pass through the obligation ledger.
- Replacing Conditional-pass obligations with canon-debt records or introducing a generalized scheduler.
- Changing source canon standing/text, Propagation reports, Admission ordering/contents, sibling obligations, or specialized pass policy beyond matching coverage.
- Temporal source-identity presentation, sibling-pass lifecycle implementation, methodology amendments, or a new ADR.
