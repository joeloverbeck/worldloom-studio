### P-01 (proposal) — Temporal/Timeline declares "Proposal mode: available" but no reachable surface can generate or preview the packet

- Severity: blocking
- Where: Temporal/Timeline destination (`docs/specs/temporal-timeline-flow.md` §31); generic Substrate → Prompt-out substrate/admin.
- What happened: The flow's own contract renders `Prompt modes — Proposal mode: available`, and its `Prompt-out preview` panel promises "Prompt packets include source manifest, current Temporal decision, date facets, latency, residue, sequence integrity, doctrine, omissions, and advisory/canon warning **for a cold external LLM**." No control on the destination generates one. Full button inventory: `Back to workflow map`, `Start or Resume Temporal`, `Refresh Temporal`, `Close Temporal Run`, `Save Temporal Coverage`, `Create or Link Temporal Timeline Card`, `Route Admission Proposal`, `Mint Temporal Debt`, `Record Governed Skip`, `Use Spatial-temporal analyst`. `document.querySelector('pre.prompt-packet-text')` → **null**. Clicking `Use Spatial-temporal analyst` produces **no visible change at all** — the panel still reads `Advisory: none`. The fallback Substrate panel is no better: its `Load Prompt Step` returned a status of `packet none · body none · source_manifest none`, and `Copy Current Packet` / `Download Current Packet` both rendered `disabled: true`. It also silently defaulted to **pressure** mode — the mode the flow had just declared *blocked* — because `promptOutActionContextFromQuery` resolves an omitted `mode` to `pressure` (`packages/server/src/prompt-out-step-actions.ts:148`) and the panel omits it. The server is not at fault: `POST /api/prompt-out/steps/actions/generate?...&mode=proposal` returns a complete 7,977-byte packet. **The UI simply never asks.** Evidence: `field-build-19-07-temporal-no-inflow-prompt-preview.png`.
- What the methodology requires: `20_ai_assisted_workflow.md` and `docs/specs/prompt-out-context-assembly.md` make prompt-out a first-class beat of every decision point, and `docs/specs/temporal-timeline-flow.md` §31 says this flow "uses the existing Prompt-out lifecycle with the `temporal_spatial_analyst` template."
- The snag: Both prompt modes are unreachable from every user-facing surface; the packet exists only behind a diagnostic API call.
- Design verdict: redesign candidate — local polish is insufficient because there is no control to polish. The in-flow panel needs the generate/preview/copy/download lifecycle the Creation flow already has; the Substrate fallback needs a mode selector rather than a silent `pressure` default.
- Recommendation: give the Temporal `Prompt-out preview` panel the Creation flow's in-flow lifecycle — an explicit `Load proposal packet` / `Load pressure packet` pair, a rendered `pre.prompt-packet-text` preview, and enabled Copy/Download bound to the current decision identity. Make `Use Spatial-temporal analyst` either do the generation or stop existing; a button that silently seeds another panel's state is worse than no button. Have the Substrate panel send an explicit `mode`.
- Repro: Open the Jon world → Workflow map → `Follow source-selected pass` on `1. Temporal/Timeline` → `Start or Resume Temporal` → inspect the `Prompt-out preview` panel and click `Use Spatial-temporal analyst`. No packet renders. Then Substrate → `Load Prompt Step` → Copy/Download are disabled and status reads `packet none`.
- Fix direction: `packages/web/src/main.tsx:6056-6066` (the panel and its no-op handler); `docs/specs/temporal-timeline-flow.md`; `packages/server/src/prompt-out-step-actions.ts:148` (mode default).
- Touches: `docs/specs/prompt-out-context-assembly.md`, `docs/worldbuilding-system/20_ai_assisted_workflow.md`, PRD #204, W-1/W-4.

### P-02 (pressure) — the pressure packet omits the steward-authored material it exists to challenge

- Severity: blocking
- Where: Temporal/Timeline, step `temporal:coverage`, mode `pressure`; packet template `temporal_spatial_analyst`.
- What happened: After 7,695 bytes of steward-authored temporal coverage were saved into PAS-1, the pressure packet generated for that exact decision contained **none of it**. Greps of the packet: `evidence corpus` ABSENT, `correlation delay` ABSENT, `institutionalized dismissal` ABSENT, `person-directed` ABSENT — and also `PRP-1`, `FAC-1`, `FAC-2`, `DEB-1`, `kernel`, `World premise` all ABSENT. A unified diff of the proposal packet against the pressure packet shows **27 changed lines, every one of them a mode label or instruction string**; the `<decision_material>` block is byte-identical between the two and holds only FAC-3's record text. The packet nevertheless instructs the reader to "treat **steward-authored material** as material under review" and asks for "pressure, risks, alternatives, and questions **on steward-authored material**." A byte-verified cold subagent (`ad6f67ab96e05c840`; submitted-payload readback `075de3ea…` / 7822, exact match) reported: "I cannot check FAC-3 for *coverage* against a timeline, because I was given no timeline. **A coverage pass with no chronology to cover against is a review of one paragraph, not a sweep**," and "I was **NOT** pressure-testing the temporal coverage of a world, because no world was shown to me." Decisive corroboration: after four coverage lenses were substantively revised, reloading the pressure packet returned a **byte-identical** packet (`075de3ea…`, 7822 bytes) — the packet is wholly insensitive to the material it claims to review. The parallel proposal probe (`a57fe62715097a128`, readback `f2893419…` / 7977, exact match) failed the same way and, unable to see PRP-1's six propagated consequences, recommended an **explicit no-card close** — the opposite of the correct outcome — while honestly hedging "my central recommendation could be wrong."
- What the methodology requires: `docs/specs/prompt-out-context-assembly.md`'s cold-LLM test — a packet must carry the context its decision needs. `20`'s pressure mode exists to challenge steward-authored material; the current decision's material is that material.
- The snag: Pressure mode is structurally incapable of pressuring anything. It ships the fact record and the instruction to review coverage, but never the coverage — nor the kernel, the propagation report that created the obligation, the sibling facts, or the open debts that define the protected boundaries.
- Design verdict: redesign candidate — this is a context-assembly defect, not copy. The packet builder must include the current pass's authored sections and the source fact's governing neighbourhood.
- Recommendation: assemble the Temporal packet from (a) the pass report's authored coverage sections for the current step, (b) the source fact plus its Propagation report (PRP-1) and gate result, (c) related canon facts and open canon debts that bound the protected mysteries, and (d) the kernel's tone/scale/consequence-mode commitments. Stop duplicating the source record byte-identically in both `<decision_material>` and `<source_records>` — the cold reader flagged that this burns roughly a quarter of the packet's world-content budget to say the same thing twice. Make `<omissions>` name the *actual* omissions; today it states the rule ("Unavailable world context must be named before copy-out rather than silently omitted") while naming nothing.
- Repro: With the Jon world open, run the Temporal pass for FAC-3, author and save coverage, then `POST /api/prompt-out/steps/actions/generate?flowKey=temporal_timeline&templateKey=temporal_spatial_analyst&recordId=6&stepKey=temporal:coverage&mode=pressure` and grep the returned `prompt` for any authored coverage phrase. None is present; the packet body hash is `075de3ea32610a9fc3116b0159e3073f908c78bc2289f2957837147871c2171f` both before and after a substantive revision.
- Fix direction: the `temporal_spatial_analyst` packet assembly in `packages/server/src/temporal-flow.ts` and the shared context assembler behind `/api/prompt-out/steps/actions/generate`.
- Touches: `docs/specs/prompt-out-context-assembly.md`, PRD #204, Field Build 16 P-01 (same shape, Propagation), W-1.

### F-03 — Temporal coverage is write-once per run, and the UI hides the server's refusal, silently losing the steward's revision

- Severity: blocking
- Where: Temporal/Timeline, `Save Temporal Coverage`, step `temporal:coverage`.
- What happened: Four coverage lenses were revised in response to pressure (visible values verified before submit) and saved. An in-page `fetch` hook captured the real response: `POST /api/temporal/coverage` → **HTTP 400** `{"error":"Temporal coverage is append-only for this run; start a new pass for corrected coverage"}`. That refusal is **never surfaced**: `serverRefusalTextAnywhereOnPage: false`, and no `[role=alert]`, toast, or banner carries it. The editors **keep displaying the discarded text**, so the steward has every reason to believe the save succeeded. Only `Refresh Temporal` reveals the truth — the fields revert to the pre-revision text, and the persisted pass report still holds the original 7,695-byte coverage, still containing the claim the steward had just corrected (`"No institution has ever correlated"` → present; `"KNOWN or GOVERNED"` → absent). Server corroboration: `packages/server/src/temporal-flow.ts:448`. **Mechanism, captured from the dev server's client log at closeout:** the rejection is never handled at all — `[vite] (client) [Unhandled rejection] ApiError: Temporal coverage is append-only for this run; start a new pass for corrected coverage`, thrown at `if (!response.ok) throw new ApiError(payload.error ?? response.statusText, response.status, payload)`. The save handler awaits the API call without a `catch`, so the 400 escapes as an unhandled promise rejection and no code path ever renders it. Evidence: `field-build-19-08-temporal-coverage-append-only-silent-loss.png`; dev log `/tmp/worldloom-field-build/dev-server-19.log` (two occurrences, 19:30:07 and 19:31:44, one per save attempt).
- What the methodology requires: `03_truth_layers_and_canon_governance.md` — canon-bearing writes are governed and auditable; a refused write must be visible as a refusal. `docs/specs/browser-visible-guidance-acceptance.md` requires server policy to be rendered, not swallowed.
- The snag: A rejected write presents as a successful one. This is silent data loss on the exact material the pressure beat is designed to improve.
- Design verdict: local polish — the server policy is already correct and explicit; the UI simply must render the 400 instead of discarding it.
- Recommendation: catch the `ApiError` in the coverage save handler (today it escapes as an unhandled rejection) and surface the server's error text in an `aria-live` region beside `Save Temporal Coverage`; keep the editors dirty-but-marked-unsaved rather than pretending success; and disable or relabel the save control once coverage is frozen so the affordance stops lying. Audit the other flow handlers for the same un-caught-rejection pattern — any refused write that reaches the client this way is invisible by construction. (The deeper fix is F-04.)
- Repro: Open the Jon world → Temporal/Timeline → `Start or Resume Temporal` (source fact FAC-3, record id 6) → author and `Save Temporal Coverage` once (succeeds) → edit any coverage field → `Save Temporal Coverage` again. The network returns 400; the screen shows nothing; the textarea keeps the lost text; `Refresh Temporal` discards it.
- Fix direction: `packages/web/src/main.tsx` Temporal coverage save handler; `docs/specs/temporal-timeline-flow.md`; `docs/specs/browser-visible-guidance-acceptance.md`.
- Touches: `docs/worldbuilding-system/03_truth_layers_and_canon_governance.md`, W-3/W-7.

### F-04 — Temporal has no revisable staging phase, so pressure-earned corrections cannot be made at all (unrevisable-material stop)

- Severity: blocking
- Where: Temporal/Timeline run lifecycle, `temporal:coverage` → `Close Temporal Run`.
- What happened: Pressure earned a substantive, correct change to the authored coverage (see P-02 and the Decision-point log). The active workflow exposes **no edit, retract, supersede, or replace path** for staged coverage within a run: coverage freezes on the **first** save, mid-run, and the server's only offered remedy is "start a new pass for corrected coverage." Taking that remedy would strand PAS-1 — an append-only, `accepted` pass report — in canon carrying coverage the steward now knows is wrong. Meanwhile the server reports **"No server-returned blockers"** and `Close Temporal Run` is enabled, so the app is mechanically ready to freeze defective material into canon. Propagation was given precisely the lifecycle Temporal lacks (revisable staging, then a single append-only close: Field Build 16 F-03 → Field Build 18 V-02, issues #353/#357/#368).
- What the methodology requires: `09_temporal_and_timeline_protocol.md` and `20`'s pressure mode both assume the steward revises material when challenge earns it; `07`/`03` reserve append-only freezing for the *close* boundary, not for the first keystroke of authoring.
- The snag: The one beat the methodology cares most about — pressure changes the material — is impossible in this flow. Mechanical close readiness and methodology readiness have come apart: the app says "ready", the method says "not ready".
- Design verdict: redesign candidate — local polish cannot add a lifecycle phase that does not exist. Temporal needs the pre-close revision model Propagation already has.
- Recommendation: give Temporal a revisable staging phase with the Propagation shape — coverage editable and re-savable (with retained lineage/revision audit rows) while the run is `in_progress`, frozen into one append-only pass report only at `Close Temporal Run`. Until then, `Close Temporal Run` should be blocked, not merely unblocked-by-silence, whenever the steward has unsaved or refused revisions. Apply the same audit to Constraint Composition and Institutional/Economic/Suppression, which are the next two obligations in this same handoff and are likely to share the defect.
- Repro: As F-03's repro; then observe that no control on the destination can amend, retract, or supersede the frozen `Coverage lenses` section, while `Close Temporal Run` remains enabled and close blockers read "No server-returned blockers."
- Fix direction: `packages/server/src/temporal-flow.ts` (coverage write policy and run lifecycle); `docs/specs/temporal-timeline-flow.md`; mirror `docs/specs/propagation-flow.md`'s staging/close boundary.
- Touches: issues #353/#357/#368, `docs/worldbuilding-system/09_temporal_and_timeline_protocol.md`, `docs/worldbuilding-system/03_truth_layers_and_canon_governance.md`, W-5/W-6/W-7.

### F-01 — A governed conditional-pass deferral is terminal: no reinstatement, and completing the pass later cannot clear it

- Severity: friction
- Where: Workflow map → Post-Propagation conditional-pass handoff (PRD #372).
- What happened: On a byte copy of the world, once an obligation is deferred with a rationale, its `Follow source-selected pass` button becomes `disabled: true`, and the handoff section contains no reinstate / undo / re-open affordance (a regex sweep for `reinstat|undo|resume|un-defer|reopen` over the section text found nothing). The steward cannot redeem a deferral through the governed surface. Source corroboration: the only write route is `/api/conditional-pass-obligations/:id/defer`, and the coverage path matches `WHERE ... disposition = 'outstanding'` (`packages/server/src/conditional-pass-obligations.ts:~261`), returning `null` when nothing matches — so even doing the real methodology work afterwards cannot mark a deferred obligation covered. Evidence: `field-build-19-06-all-governed-admission-primary.png`.
- What the methodology requires: `07_propagation_engine.md` treats a deferred pass as *governed debt*, not abandonment — debt is meant to be redeemable. `03` requires the read side to keep telling the truth about what the world owes.
- The snag: Deferral is a one-way door. A steward who defers Temporal/Timeline today and does the work next month is left with an obligation permanently marked `deferred`, a pass report unlinked to it, and a map that misreports the world's real state.
- Design verdict: local polish — the ledger, provenance, and history model already exist; what is missing is a reinstate action and a coverage path that accepts a previously deferred obligation.
- Recommendation: add a `Reinstate obligation` action mirroring `Defer with rationale` (explicit, rationale-bearing, appended to the same governed history), and let the coverage path match `disposition IN ('outstanding','deferred')` so completing the real pass clears the debt and records which report covered it.
- Fix direction: `packages/server/src/conditional-pass-obligations.ts`; `packages/server/src/http/conditional-pass-obligation-routes.ts`; the handoff panel in `packages/web/src/main.tsx`.
- Touches: PRD #372, `docs/specs/workflow-map-and-navigation.md`, `docs/worldbuilding-system/07_propagation_engine.md`, W-9/W-10.
