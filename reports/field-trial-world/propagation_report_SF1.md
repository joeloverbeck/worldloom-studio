# Propagation Report — SF-1 Memory-Salt Sealing

Per `templates/propagation_report.md`; sweep per `checklists/propagation_sweep.md`.

## Fact

Memory-salt sealing as admitted (fact card SF-1, constraint suite S1a–f, one-in-twenty yield).

## Severity level

Major (Level 3 capability → major work per `06` mapping).

## Consequence mode

Material/institutional; lyrical-symbolic secondary (wet/dry).

## Initial scope

Baseline continuity; present era, practice centuries old; all Saltmarrow; unequal access by class and coast.

## Shock-cone order

### Immediate effects

Deathbeds are performances with stakes: the naming of the taster is the last exercise of power. Households organize around the cellar. A person's "salt" (whether they have a grain waiting) is a status fact as basic as land.

### Prerequisites exposed

Harvest licensing and neap-tide cutting craft; assaying as a profession with apprenticeship; grain-paper manufacture; the deathbed witness custom predating the Assay (the Assay codified, did not invent).

### Domain ripples (14-domain pass; quiet domains declared)

1. **Physics/metaphysics/cosmology.** Memory can outlive its person exactly once. Theology splits on whether the grain holds the memory or the person's *witness* of it; no sect claims the soul is in the salt (that heresy — "grain-souling" — was suppressed two centuries back; residue: its name survives as an insult). The Shelf's mechanism is mystery-bounded; metaphysical speculation exists but cannot experiment (evidence-missing set closed).
2. **Geography/climate/infrastructure.** Estuary chokepoint; the Stair (the licensed approach to the Shelf); dry-vault architecture inland of flood lines; the humid climate makes dryness engineering expensive — cellar quality is a visible class marker.
3. **Ecology/food/disease.** Declared quiet (Law 10): brine ecology exists but bears no load. One residue kept: estuary fish are never salted with Shelf salt — culinary taboo, practical waste.
4. **Population/demography/household.** Inheritance practice: wills name tasters and alternates; disputes over "unsound sealing" (delirium) are the dominant probate litigation. Care of the dying professionalizes (sitters who keep grains dry and formulas ready). The unsalted majority (19 of 20) have mirror-rites: "dry deaths" with spoken memory bequests — the poor imitate the rich's capability in words.
5. **Production/labor/technology.** Professions: cutters (licensed), assayers, sitters, brokers, vault-wrights, wet-runners (smugglers). Guild secrets: stamp metallurgy, humidity craft. No automation pressure (mode-consistent).
6. **Economy/trade/scarcity.** Grains as the supreme store of value (durable, scarce, unforgeable-in-substance but forgeable-in-paper). Brokerage, cellar insurance, transfer-tax revenue as a state pillar. **Flag → constraint composition:** durable + scarce + taxable-on-transfer + verifiable-only-by-destruction composes into collateral use — cellar-banks lending against grains they can never verify. Assigned to stage 5 (`08`).
7. **Governance/law/bureaucracy.** The registry as the state's memory of memories; probate courts; Single Witness doctrine (court-appointed taster testifies once, under oath, corroboration required); inspection stops at the cellar door without probate cause. Crimes: dry-lying, stamp forgery, taster coercion, "drowning" a cellar (aggravated — destroying a family's dead).
8. **War/coercion/security.** The Pan Wars as the fact's military fossil (fought over the source, not the capability). Doctrine: no battlefield use — a dying soldier's grain is a letter home, and armies employ salt-chaplains to complete seals; captured cellars are war booty with atrocity weight. Deterrence residue: coastal forts guard the Stair, not the towns.
9. **Religion/ritual/myth.** Sea-sects (the memory belongs to the water; sealing is theft — they die dry on principle); Assay-adjacent orthodoxy (sealing as stewardship); the Shelf's own sacred complex (mystery ledger). Funerary rites split by whether a grain was sealed.
10. **Culture/custom/language/identity.** "Keep it dry"; "salt-eyed"; "he never got his grain" (a wasted life); white-grain taboo; the tongue-thumb funeral gesture; Lee Shore dry tables; naming a taster as the strongest kinship signal that exists.
11. **Knowledge/education/records.** Assayer apprenticeships; the registry as historiographic instrument (tasted grains enter chronicle only through the Single Witness's sworn account — history has a chain-of-custody problem the world *knows about*); proof standards: sworn taster testimony ranks above documents for events, below them for numbers.
12. **History/memory/path dependence.** The Concession structure; the failed judicial ordeal at the Shelf; the grain-souling suppression; pre-war salts as heirloom-contraband; the registry's founding as the war's administrative residue.
13. **Daily life/material residue.** Cellars, grain-papers, punched stamps, sitters' satchels, humidity charms (folk, inert, ubiquitous), children forbidden to play "sealing" with kitchen salt (they do anyway; the game is called Last Words).
14. **Aesthetics/tone/narrative use.** Deepens the ledger-and-oilskin identity; wet/dry motif governs blessing, sabotage, and rite; the capability resists spectacle structurally (one grain, one taster, once — no set-piece scale available). Genre contract intact.

**Negative domain check:** none suspicious — inheritance, evidence, war, and religion all show adaptation. (The atlas's negative-domain example — "cheap resurrection but unchanged inheritance law" — was the exact failure this world was most at risk of; the domain-4/7 rows answer it.)

### Institutional or mode-equivalent adaptations

Assay of Griefs; probate courts + Single Witness doctrine; license families; diver-wardens (sacred complex); sea-sect counter-institution; cellar-banks (emergent, stage 5).

### Economic or value-pressure adaptations

See domains 6/7. Black-market: wet-running, stolen-grain fencing (spent-secret problem makes fencing peculiar: value collapses on first taste, so stolen grains sell as *threats* — "I have your father's memory" — extortion is the true theft economy). 

### Enemy adaptations

Extortion-by-grain; taster coercion; dry-lying at scale after disasters (mass-grief fraud); Lee Shore revanchism financing itself by attestation forgery (see contradiction below).

### Temporal residue

Centuries-old: fossils listed in domain 12. Latency check: all present institutions are old enough to be weathered — none may present as newly invented at QA.

### Spatial diffusion

Single source; export taxed at the estuary custom-house; inland Saltmarrow experiences the capability mainly as probate news and chronicle disputes; abroad, grains are curiosities priced as relics — no foreign institutional adaptation (distance + scarcity + skepticism explain "why not everywhere").

### Character pressure

The named-and-dreading taster; the passed-over sibling; the sitter who has heard a thousand formulas; the assayer who can no longer attend deathbeds unprofessionally; the Lee Shore matriarch guarding a pre-war cellar; the broker who matches strangers' griefs.

### Aesthetic and preservation residue

Wet/dry motif; the sacred register held apart at the Shelf (preservation boundary respected: nothing in this report explains or exploits the Shelf).
