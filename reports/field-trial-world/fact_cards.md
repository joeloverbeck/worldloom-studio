## Card SF-1 — Memory-salt sealing

**Fact statement.** A dying person can seal exactly one chosen memory into a grain of unrefined Weeping Shelf salt; one living taster may dissolve it once and live the memory from the dier's perspective; the grain is consumed. (Constraint suite S1a–S1f admitted with it.)

**Working scope.** Baseline continuity; present era, practice centuries old; all Saltmarrow, source at the Shelf; all populations, unequal access.
**Fact type.** Capability (+ constraints). **Consequence mode.** Material/institutional; lyrical-symbolic secondary. **Truth layer.** Objective canon. **Canon status.** Accepted with constraints. **Constraint tags.** Rare, costly, geography-bound, ritual-bound, monopolized (certification), unstable (moisture).
**Prerequisites.** Hard: the Shelf and its salt property. Social: deathbed witnessing custom. Institutional (soft): Assay for lawful use. Economic: harvest/licensing structure. Aesthetic: understated register (no spectacle).
**Costs and limits.** See capability card; load-bearing: one-in-twenty yield, selectivity of the chosen memory, verification-by-consumption.
**Shock-cone summary.** Immediate: deathbed practice, naming conflicts. Prerequisites exposed: harvest licensing, assaying craft. Second-order: probate testimony doctrine, grief brokerage, cellar architecture. Institutional adaptations: Assay, courts, insurers. Economic: grain market, black market, tax base. Countermeasures: forgery detection, skeptic sects. Fossils: Pan Wars, pre-war salts, saltless cuisine. Distant echoes: banking pressure (→ constraint composition).
**Evidence and belief.** Everyone knows the capability is real; disputes concern particular grains (provenance, sound sealing, selective truth). Dry-liars exploit the desperate.
**Contradiction risks.** Court use vs. consumption (resolved as Single Witness doctrine); registry claims vs. pre-war salts (→ flagged to contradiction sweep).
**Mystery / wonder risks.** Must not explain the Shelf; salt's efficacy is *used*, never *derived*.
**Admission decision operation.** Constrain. **Repair operation.** None.
**Notes.** The selectivity limit (dier chooses the memory) was the single most generative admission decision: it keeps memory honest as experience yet partial as testimony, which powers the court, kinship, and mystery pressures without breaking tone.
**Non-naturalistic consequence prompt.** Declared route: symbolic residue (wet/dry motif). Mode-equivalent consequence: dryness = keeping, wetness = release/loss recurs in blessing, sabotage, and rite. Decorative-one-off risk: if wet/dry never governs a choice, it is decoration — watched at QA.

## Card S2b — Uncertified salt is contraband

**Fact statement.** Possession of an uncertified memory-salt is finable; sale is criminal, throughout Saltmarrow jurisdiction.
**Working scope.** Baseline; since the Concession era (post-war codification); weaker enforcement on the Lee Shore.
**Fact type.** Law. **Consequence mode.** Material/institutional. **Truth layer.** Objective canon. **Status.** Accepted. **Tags.** Jurisdictional.
**Prerequisites.** Assay registry (S2a); state enforcement capacity.
**Costs and limits.** Enforcement costs; inspection powers stop at the household cellar door without probate cause — the privacy line that makes smuggling livable.
**Shock-cone summary (abbrev.).** Black market priced by certification risk; forged papers; informers; Lee Shore resentment; a smuggler profession ("wet-runners").
**Evidence and belief.** Public knows the law; public belief overestimates Assay detection ability (useful to the Assay; quietly false).
**Contradiction risks.** Interaction with S3b (legal-to-keep pre-war salts) — how do you prove a salt is pre-war? → flagged to contradiction sweep.
**Admission decision operation.** Institutionalize. **Repair.** None. **Notes.** The proof-of-age question is deliberate story fuel, not an oversight — recorded as evidence debt.

## Card S3a — The Concession of Weirs

**Fact statement.** The Pan Wars ended ninety years ago in the Concession of Weirs, stripping Lee Shore towns of Shelf harvest rights in perpetuity and vesting hereditary licenses in Weir Coast families.
**Working scope.** Baseline; ninety years ago; both coasts.
**Fact type.** Event / history. **Consequence mode.** Material/institutional. **Truth layer.** Objective canon. **Status.** Accepted. **Tags.** Era-bound (event); its legal effects current.
**Prerequisites.** The salt was worth a war (SF-1 value ✓); pre-war shared harvest custom (implied — residue debt: what was the older custom? assigned to temporal pass).
**Costs and limits.** "In perpetuity" is a treaty word, not a physical law — revanchism is structurally possible.
**Shock-cone summary (abbrev.).** License aristocracy; Lee Shore grievance institutions; asymmetric commemoration; pre-war salts as sacred contraband-adjacent heirlooms.
**Evidence and belief.** The treaty text is objective; *why* the war started is already mythologized differently per coast (mythic truth, both versions canon as beliefs).
**Contradiction risks.** Pre-war salt provenance (with S2b) — flagged.
**Admission decision operation.** Historicize. **Repair.** None.

## Card S4b — The nine-day silence

**Fact statement.** Anyone who passes fully beneath the Weeping Shelf returns physically unharmed and mute for exactly nine days. No recorded exceptions.
**Working scope.** Baseline; as old as memory; the Shelf only.
**Fact type.** Metaphysical rule (site-bound). **Consequence mode.** Sacred. **Truth layer.** Objective canon (the phenomenon; its meaning is protected — see mystery ledger). **Status.** Accepted with constraints. **Tags.** Geography-bound, sacred.
**Prerequisites.** None knowable — that absence is the point, governed as boundary, not gap.
**Costs and limits.** Nine days of silence is a real cost (livelihood, testimony, command); exact and invariant — the world can *rely* on it, which is what makes institutions try to use it.
**Shock-cone summary (abbrev.).** Rites of deliberate diving (penance, mourning); a rejected judicial ordeal (courts tried compelling accused liars to dive; the silence proves nothing and the practice died — historical residue, one century back); diver-wardens who police the approach; "gone under" as idiom for the unreachable.
**Evidence and belief.** Everyone coastal knows it is real; interpretations plural and contested (S4d).
**Contradiction risks.** Any future scene where a diver speaks early, or describes the beneath — the boundary's tripwire.
**Mystery / wonder risks.** High by design — all governed in `mystery_ledger_weeping_shelf.md`.
**Admission decision operation.** Constrain. **Repair.** None.
**Non-naturalistic consequence prompt.** Route: taboo + ritual condition + dread. Mode-equivalent consequence: the silence functions as the Shelf's tax; rites form around paying it voluntarily. Decorative risk: none — it already governs institutions.
