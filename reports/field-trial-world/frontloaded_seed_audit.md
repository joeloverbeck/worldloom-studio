## Consequence audit (summary per seed cluster)

- **SF-1:** Must already be true: the Shelf predates society; deathbed practice is old. Immediate changes: inheritance law, evidence practice, grief economy. Institutions: the Assay (SF-2 answers this). Economies: memory-brokerage, cellar-security, harvest licensing. Ordinary residue: cellars, stamps, blessings, taboos (kernel). Counter-adaptation: forgers of assay stamps, "dry-liars" who sell inert refined salt to the desperate, skeptic sects who refuse tasting. Historical fossils: pre-war salts, the Concession. Mysteries protected: none inside SF-1. Latency: practice is centuries old — world already adapted; **the audit question "why is the world not already different" inverts here: the world must look already-different, which propagation must supply.**
- **SF-2:** Presupposes literacy/registry practice and state enforcement. Enemies: Lee Shore smugglers, forger rings, anti-Assay pietists who hold sealing too sacred to tax.
- **SF-3:** Presupposes the salt was worth a war (consistent with SF-1 value). Residue: asymmetric commemoration, saltless cuisine, license aristocracy. Latency: 90 years = two generations of grievance institutionalization.
- **SF-4:** Protected. Observable effects fixed (weep, nine-day silence, no descriptions). Counter-adaptation the mode demands: institutions must have *tried* to use the silence (penance-diving, judicial ordeal proposals) and been governed by rite instead. Kernel's non-naturalistic prompt already flagged the deliberate-diver problem — carried into admission as SF-4's first pressure line.
