# Mystery / Wonder Ledger Entry — The Weeping Shelf

Per `templates/mystery_ledger_entry.md`. Covers S4a (cause of weeping), S4c (content of the time beneath), S4d (belief ecology). S4b (the nine-day silence as phenomenon) is objective canon on its own fact card.

## Protected effect type

Hybrid: sacred opacity (primary) + governed mystery (secondary, only insofar as characters treat it as a question).

## Puzzle question, if any

None at the authorial level — the world may ask "why does the Shelf weep?" but the design refuses to treat it as a puzzle with a findable answer. (Characters' question exists; the *design's* answer does not.)

## Mystery type, if applicable

Authorial mystery boundary (metaphysical, if forced to classify).

## What is fixed

The Shelf weeps brine continuously and has for all recorded memory. Its salt takes memory (SF-1). Passing fully beneath causes exactly nine days of muteness, no exceptions, no physical harm. Returned divers say there is nothing to tell, and no diver contradicts another.

## What is author-secret

Nothing. This is the load-bearing choice: there is no hidden explanation file.

## What is deliberately undecided

Why the Shelf weeps; what, if anything, the time beneath contains; whether "nothing to tell" is inability, prohibition, or accurate report. Undecidability is part of the design — the steward has committed not to know.

## What is forbidden

Any origin story at objective or author-secret layer; any instrument, rite, or capability that retrieves content from beneath; any diver who describes the beneath or breaks silence early; any pricing or mechanization of the silence (no institution may successfully sell, compel, or weaponize it); any adaptation that renders the beneath visually.

## Truth-layer boundaries

- Objective canon: the weep, the salt property, the nine-day silence, divers' consistent "nothing to tell."
- Mystery boundary: cause of weeping; content of the beneath.
- Local beliefs (all canon *as beliefs*, none confirmed): Lee Shore — the Shelf weeps for the drowned and silence is penance; Weir Coast chapels — the silence is audience with something that listens; divers' families — it is mercy (you are spared what you saw); children's lore — the Shelf is digesting your words.
- Mythic truth: each coast's account of why the sea gave salt memory (organizes rites; literal accuracy refused).
- Propaganda: Assay-era pamphlets once claimed the Shelf favors the licensing families; discredited, survives as a Weir Coast toast.
- Honest error: naturalist attempts to explain the weep as aquifer seepage — plausible, repeatedly published inland, and quietly unable to explain the salt or the silence.

## Preservation boundary

The Shelf must remain larger than explanation: the one thing in a mercantile world that cannot be bought, taxed, assayed, or made to testify. Its register (sacred) must never blur into the ledger register (institutional).

## Who knows partial truth

No one — including the steward. Divers hold whatever the beneath is, and hold it as silence; whether that constitutes knowledge is itself protected.

## False beliefs

The four interpretations above; the aquifer theory; the discredited license-favor doctrine. Consequences: pilgrimage economies on both coasts, penance-diving, chapel authority, inland skepticism that coastal people find blasphemous.

## Evidence available

The weep itself; salinity gradient; the invariant nine days; the behavioral change in some returned divers (quieter, unhurried — "salt-eyed" overlaps in slang); centuries of parish silence-records kept by diver-wardens.

## Evidence missing

Anything from beneath: no sample taken under the Shelf holds memory or differs measurably; no testimony; no instrument returns readings (boundary: expeditions fail mundanely — funding, weather, nerve — never dramatically; the world declines the question without theater).

## Evidence habitat

Landscape (the Shelf, the approach stairs), ritual (warden rites, penance dives), parish silence-records, gossip and children's lore, cuisine (Lee Shore dry tables sit inside this belief ecology).

## Consequences already visible

Diver-wardens as a small institution; the failed judicial ordeal (courts once compelled accused liars to dive; abandoned a century ago — its residue is the idiom "let the Shelf try him," now meaning an unanswerable case); pilgrimage inns; the prohibition on harvesting beneath; mourning dives after mass drownings.

## Recurrence / motif / transformation

The silence recurs as motif in the world's ordinary language: courts "go under" when deadlocked; a kept secret is "held beneath the Shelf"; nine-day mourning periods echo the silence even for unsalted deaths.

## Reveal permissions

None at origin level, ever, in any branch or medium. Permitted: new *interpretive communities*, new rites, new false theories, new residue — the belief ecology may grow without limit.

## Reveal prohibitions

The cause of the weep; the content of the beneath; early speech; a diver's description. Also prohibited: an author-secret file "for safekeeping" — deciding is itself the breach.

## Explanation-pressure operation

Consecrate (primary), forbid (supporting).

## What would break if solved or flattened

The kernel's core promise ("the sea keeps exactly one secret it will not sell"); the sacred register that gives the mercantile register its outside; the meaning of every penance dive and mourning rite retroactively collapses into mechanism.

- [x] No protected effect hides an ordinary contradiction (the failed ordeal is governed history, not a papered-over conflict).

## Sacred-opacity accountability (required)

- **Refused explanation:** the origin and cause of the weeping; the nature/content of the time beneath; the mechanism linking Shelf salt to memory.
- **Consequences still fixed:** salt efficacy rules (S1a–f); the invariant nine days; muteness with no physical harm; divers' uniform "nothing to tell"; warden institution; failed-ordeal history; pilgrimage and mourning economies; spatial limit (the Shelf alone); no temporal drift.
- **Recurrence / boundary rule:** the earned pattern is *invariance* — the Shelf never improvises. An exception (a diver who speaks on day six, a second weeping cliff) would be arbitrary authorial convenience unless admitted as a Level 5 change with full contradiction machinery. The boundary is auditable precisely because the phenomenon is exact.
- **Contradiction implications:** any prior or future scene with an early-speaking diver, a described beneath, a memory-holding salt from elsewhere, or a successful instrumental use of the silence requires repair before publication.
- **Critique still allowed:** clarity (is the nine-day rule ever fudged?), recurrence (does the motif actually recur or is it claimed?), tone (does sacred register leak into ledger register?), cost (do dives cost something real?), evidence (are the parish silence-records consistent?), audience trust (has the opacity ever been used to dodge a promised consequence?).
- **Legitimate-vs-cheat note:** this is earned opacity because the world pays for it continuously — the silence taxes real institutions (courts lost an ordeal, wardens spend lives policing the approach, families lose nine days of a survivor's voice) and the refusal to explain closes no plot conveniently: nothing in the world's conflicts is solved by the Shelf remaining unexplained. A cheat would let the opacity absorb contradictions or excuse missing consequence; here every observable is fixed and auditable.
