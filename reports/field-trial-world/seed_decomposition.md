# Seed Decomposition — Saltmarrow

Per `05_creation_protocol.md` Phase 2. Broad kernel facts split into atomic seeds. Admission order follows the protocol's prescribed sequence (constraints → capabilities → strategic resources → public institutions → historical traumas → protected mysteries → aesthetic promises).

## SF-1 (capability: memory-salt) →

- **S1a** — Sealing works only in the act of dying; it completes at death. A healthy person's attempt does nothing.
- **S1b** — One memory per death, chosen by the dier, spoken-witnessed at the deathbed (the formula names a taster or names "the cellar").
- **S1c** — Only unrefined salt cut from the Weeping Shelf can hold a memory. Refined salt, foreign salt, or Shelf salt dissolved outside a living mouth is inert or wasted.
- **S1d** — One taster, once. Dissolving the grain on a living tongue plays the memory from the dier's perspective; the grain is consumed.
- **S1e** — The taster's recall of a tasted memory decays like any lived memory. No replay, no transcription fidelity, no copies.
- **S1f** — A sealed grain keeps indefinitely if dry. Moisture ruins it irrecoverably.

## SF-2 (institution: Assay of Griefs) →

- **S2a** — The Assay certifies and registers memory-salts (dier, witnesses, date, named taster or cellar), at the deathbed when summoned or post-hoc on inspection.
- **S2b** — Uncertified memory-salt is contraband: possession fined, sale criminal.
- **S2c** — The Assay taxes transfers (inheritance, sale, brokerage), not the sealing act itself.
- **S2d** — The Assay does not harvest. Harvest rights are hereditary licenses held by Weir Coast families under the post-war settlement.

## SF-3 (historical trauma: Pan Wars) →

- **S3a** — The Pan Wars ended ninety years ago in the Concession of Weirs: Lee Shore towns stripped of harvest rights in perpetuity.
- **S3b** — Lee Shore families still hold pre-war sealed salts: legal to keep and taste within the family, illegal to sell.
- **S3c** — Commemoration is asymmetric: Weir Coast tasting-festivals versus Lee Shore saltless cuisine and dry-table hospitality.

## SF-4 (protected effect: the Shelf) →

- **S4a** — The Shelf weeps brine continuously. The cause is unknown to every community in the world, and the steward has deliberately not decided it.
- **S4b** — Anyone who passes fully beneath the Shelf returns physically unharmed and mute for exactly nine days. No recorded exceptions.
- **S4c** — After the nine days, returned divers do not describe the time beneath. They consistently say there is nothing to tell.
- **S4d** — Communities interpret the silence differently (penance, audience, mercy, digestion); no interpretation is confirmed at any layer.

## Admission order applied

1. Constraints first: S1a, S1c, S1e, S1f (these are the limits that keep SF-1 from dominating).
2. Capability: S1b + S1d (the capability proper).
3. Strategic resource: Shelf salt as single-source strategic good (implicit in S1c + S2d).
4. Public institutions: S2a–S2d.
5. Historical trauma: S3a–S3c.
6. Protected mysteries: S4a–S4d.
7. Aesthetic promises: kernel aesthetic limits (briny, understated; sacred register reserved for the Shelf).

## Proportionality decision

Full admission gate + fact card for the highest-pressure package (SF-1 as a composed capability with its constraint seeds). Standard-severity admission for SF-2 and SF-3. SF-4 admitted through the mystery-governance path with a mystery ledger entry. Reason recorded per README "Minimal discipline": every skip or lightening is visible.
