# Contradiction Report — C-1: Pre-War Salts

Per `templates/contradiction_report.md` and `13_contradiction_retcon_and_mystery.md`. Detected by the propagation sweep (see `propagation_report_SF1.md`); constraint-leakage input from `constraint_composition_cellar_banks.md`.

## Contradiction statement

- S2b: "Possession of an uncertified memory-salt is finable; sale is criminal" — jurisdiction-wide, codified since the Concession era.
- S3b: "Lee Shore families still hold pre-war sealed salts: legal to keep and taste within the family, illegal to sell."

Pre-war salts necessarily predate the registry, and S2a certification requires deathbed recording — impossible for diers ninety years dead. As admitted, every pre-war salt is uncertified, hence finable under S2b, hence *not* "legal to keep" as S3b states. Both cannot be true in the same layer, place, time, and scope.

## Affected truth layers

Both objective canon (legal facts). No layer separation available as an escape — this is not a claims-vs-facts conflict.

## Affected scope

Lee Shore primarily (largest pre-war holdings), but any pre-registry salt anywhere; present era; probate courts, Assay inspectors, cellar-banks.

## Contradiction type

Direct factual (at the legal layer), with institutional character (a lawful practice exists without the certifying machinery that the law's own wording requires).

## Who can notice?

Everyone in-world with a pre-war grain or a badge — this is not an exotic edge case; it is every Lee Shore matriarch's daily legal condition. Audience: immediately, if both facts are ever shown. Steward: caught at propagation, pre-publication.

## Severity

Moderate (work scale): confined to two facts' wording plus one new institution, but load-bearing for the world's central grievance.

## Higher-authority material

S3a/S3b. The Concession settlement and the survival of Lee Shore heirloom cellars are kernel-derived (the grievance's liveness depends on families keeping what they can no longer make). S2b's blanket wording is the junior, erroneous formulation — admission-stage drafting error, honestly recorded.

## Mystery / protected-effect relationship

None. This conflict protects nothing; it is an ordinary legal contradiction and must not be papered over with "the law is mysterious" (the forbidden move, `13`). The Shelf complex is untouched by the repair.

## Repair operation

**Clarify scope** (primary): S2b is reworded — "Possession of a memory-salt sealed after the founding of the registry without certification is finable; sale of any uncertified salt is criminal."

**Institutionalize** (component, per `13` repairs-must-be-propagated): lawful pre-registry salts are defined by the **Concession Rolls** — the Concession's own enrollment of then-existing family cellars, sworn in the treaty year. A pre-war salt is lawful to keep iff it appears on a family's roll; roll extracts are the proof instrument.

## Retcon cost

Back-office repair; no audience-facing material exists yet. Continuity cost: reword card S2b (done below). Institutional cost: a new record type (rolls) with its own fraud surface — paid willingly, see propagation. Character/mystery/aesthetic/future costs: none, none, none (ledger texture deepened, if anything), future stories gain material.

## Propagation required

- **Roll forgery** ("salting the date") becomes the premium document crime — every stolen post-war grain wants to launder itself as pre-war.
- **Burned-roll families:** some Lee Shore rolls were destroyed in the war's last fires; families with true pre-war salts and no proof now exist as a governed evidence debt — deliberately story-rich, assigned to the uncertainty/evidence pass.
- **Cellar-banks** (stage 5 interaction): banks refuse pre-war collateral without a roll extract; the foreclosure-laundering loophole narrows to forged-roll collateral — the black market moves from salt to paper. Constraint composition record updated by reference.
- **Assay inspection practice:** inspectors carry roll extracts on Lee Shore circuits; the extract-request at a door is the scene where state and grievance meet — ordinary-life residue of the repair itself.
