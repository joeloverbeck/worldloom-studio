# Institutional / Economic / Suppression Pass — Carillon

## Arena card 1 — The Registry on a morrow

- **Arena:** the civil Registry's public counter on the day after a Rest.
- **Trigger:** every morrow (roughly one per four years, plus the Still Season's long shadow in precedent).
- **Actors:** registrants (midwives with Rest-born infants, families of Rest-dead, couples wed in the quiet), Registry clerks, Registry advocate-notaries, day-brokers loitering to intercept awkward cases, a Censorate observer (informal but customary).
- **Positions:** declarant / clerk / senior clerk (reinterpretation authority) / observer.
- **Actions:** declare, refuse to date, refer upward, refer to the Blind Window (post-C2), turn away, quietly advise a broker.
- **Information:** the clerk cannot lawfully know what day the event happened if it happened in the quiet; the declarant cannot lawfully say. The whole arena runs on **mutually performed ignorance** — the purest example of `12`'s "performing legibility while hiding the real practice."
- **Control:** senior clerks certify; the Censorate observer chills; the Office holds interpretation.
- **Costs and incentives:** declarants risk voiding the event; clerks risk sanction for dating the undatable; brokers profit from every refusal.
- **Rules-in-use vs formal rules:** formal — undated acts do not exist (Card 5). In use, *before repair*: clerks improvised morrow-dating for births "because the babies are visibly here," each improvisation technically an offense — **this gap is the formal detonation of debt D-1: an institutional contradiction (rules-in-use systematically violate the law because the law as written is unlivable). Escalated to the contradiction machinery → `contradiction_report_C1.md`.**
- **Outcomes:** common — patched registrations; rare — a voided marriage weaponized in an inheritance war; catastrophic (pre-repair) — a court forced to rule on whether a quiet-born person exists.
- **Externalities:** the quiet-born themselves, and every family whose one awkward morrow follows them for generations.
- **Repair questions:** answered by C-1/C-2 (see reports; Card 5 History).

## Arena card 2 — An assertion trial before the Censors

- **Arena:** the Censorate bench, hearing a charge of public assertion against the Calendar.
- **Trigger:** rare by design — the Censorate's product is that this arena almost never convenes.
- **Actors:** the accused, two witnesses (statutory minimum), Censors, the licensing clerk who certifies the accused's astronomy license (if any), the crowd.
- **Information:** everyone in the room may privately know the assertion is true; its truth is inadmissible — the *only* question is whether the words were said publicly. Truth is not a defense; raising it is a second count.
- **Costs and incentives:** for the Office, every trial is an advertisement of the thing it prosecutes — hence the rules-in-use: charges are almost always converted to license revocations, contraband fines (S4b), or quiet exile *before* trial. A full public assertion trial is the Office losing.
- **Rules-in-use:** prosecution by attrition — fines, de-licensing, tenancy pressure through Office-friendly landlords — with trial as the never-used last step. The unaffordability doctrine in operation (Card 6).
- **Outcomes:** catastrophic branch (named, not developed): a defendant wealthy and foreign-backed enough to *afford* the trial and subpoena the feast registry. The Office's nightmare has a name in chancery circles abroad: "the calendar case."

## Institution card — The Office of the Kalends

- **Purpose:** govern lawful time; conceal the founding error; keep the doctrine's cost of collapse unpayable.
- **Origin:** Era II–III (the Held Breath and the Settlement) — an emergency that never ended (`temporal_pass.md`).
- **Rules-in-use:** the college schedules by the Long Count while teaching the doctrine even internally — new Intercalators learn the arithmetic in stages, each stage presented as the last (the Office suppresses *inward* as well as outward; its own junior censors do not know the drift is real).
- **Roles:** the nine chairs (Intercalators); the Censorate; the stamp office; feast-clerk placements inside the Temple (see D-6 below). Excluded: everyone — there is no citizen relationship to the Office except obedience.
- **Resources controlled:** the schedule, the almanac monopoly, archive access licenses, bell command.
- **Enforcement:** the Censorate's attrition machinery; the Ringers' oath; archive licensing.
- **Information:** knows the drift, the Long Count, the schedule; conceals its Year-36 measurements (D-4) behind archive licensing — the reading-room rule-in-use is that *requesting the Year 30–50 quarantine shelf* marks the requester, and patient clerks who read too well are promoted into the Office (capture as suppression: the archive's best threats become its staff).
- **Economy:** licensing fees, stamp revenue, and the recurring cost it *pays*: one day of city commerce per Rest — the Office is the rare institution that taxes itself to maintain its lie.
- **Legitimacy:** the Founders' doctrine; the Rest's genuine popularity (debt mercy, rest, blessing); three centuries of the sky seeming to agree.
- **Failure modes:** the constitutional gap (no audit); interpretive overreach into the Temple; the Long Count's widening secret; the corrosion of Censors who spend careers pricing a truth they cannot say.
- **Counter-institutions:** below.
- **Aesthetic residue:** grey facings, the unstamped-page test at booksellers, the Office's proverbial politeness ("the Kalends never shout — they reschedule").

## Counter-institution card — The day-brokers

- **Official institution opposed/bypassed:** the Registry and courts (Card 5's law); functionally licensed by the Office's tolerance (S3c).
- **Reason for existence:** the law manufactures demand for deniability and then refuses to adjudicate it.
- **Users:** merchants settling grey debts; families unmaking betrothals; duelists; anyone rich enough to need a day that never happened.
- **Services:** venue, witnesses-who-are-not-witnesses, custody of tokens ("quiet pledges" — physical objects exchanged in lieu of datable signatures), memory of obligations no court will hold.
- **Trust mechanism:** reputation staked across generations of brokerage houses; the cant; ruinous informal blacklisting ("a broker who dates is dead to the quiet").
- **Enforcement:** no violence guild — the brokers' sanction is *exposure by implication*: a cheat finds his other quiet dealings suddenly rememberable by everyone. (Sweep's black-market question "how is violence organized?" answered by its absence: the brokers replaced violence with mutually assured dating.)
- **Risks / predation threshold:** the trade turns predatory exactly where it touches persons rather than property — quiet marriages and quiet disinheritances (C-2's fuel). Post-C2, brokers who arrange status acts are outside even the tolerance.
- **Suppression and adaptation:** the Office tolerates, observes, and occasionally harvests the brokers as informants; brokers survive by being more useful unprosecuted.
- **Parasites:** **calendar touts** — sellers of schedule foreknowledge (real or invented). Touts are hated by the brokers (leaks concentrate Censorate attention), hunted by the Office as a counter-espionage matter (military secret, per propagation report domain 8), and yet structurally inevitable: every Office clerk with debts is a potential tout. Feedback loop per `07` mechanism 9, running at full strength.

**Sun-counters (inline treatment):** the exile counter-institution proper — foreign-hosted, publishing true ephemerides, smuggling them in as devotional calendars (S4c). Official failure that created them: the sedition law's conversion of a scholarly correction into an identity. Predation threshold: when smuggling networks start moving more than almanacs (the tables ride established contraband routes, and the routes do not care what they carry).

## Economy loop (per `12` Part II)

- **Scarcity:** undated time (manufactured, four days a decade); secrecy tiers; archive access; true ephemerides.
- **Prices:** broker fees (steep, customary, never invoiced); tout tips; conversion clerks' fees at the ports; Rest-eve premiums; the state's self-tax of one day's commerce.
- **Substitution:** the quiet replaced older deniability tech (midnight, back rooms) with something better — state-guaranteed nonexistence; the old informal-deniability trades withered accordingly.
- **Complements:** memory itself became valuable — broker-house memory, elder tallies, the feast registry; anything that *remembers across the quiet* commands a premium.
- **Surplus capture:** brokers, touts, conversion clerks, the Temple (feast revenue), Office fee offices.
- **Externalities:** quiet-born persons; provincial desync (D-3); every honest trade that loses a day.
- **Commons problem (sweep catch):** **the credibility of the count is a commons.** Every tout leak, broker excess, and clerk improvisation grazes it; the Office monitors defection like a commons regulator, and its tolerance thresholds (S3c) are the local rules that prevent collapse. This framing — the lie as a managed commons — is the pass's synthesis-level insight.
- **Households:** rent and interest customs; dowries specify the count they run on; children taught the noon breath before they are taught why.

## Suppression audit

- **Methods in use:** censorship, licensing, credential control, controlled vocabulary ("the quiet," "the Rest," never "the correction"), monopolized education, selective enforcement, interpretive capture (below), sacrilege charges (false Rests), promotion-as-capture (archive clerks).
- **Residues (all verifiable in earlier artifacts):** euphemism ecology; missing archives (D-4's marked shelf); strange court procedure (truth inadmissible); families who will not speak of a morrow; broker cant; children's warnings; exile community; the almost-right rumor ("the bells mourn the unnumbered dead").
- **D-4 disposition:** the Year-36 measurements survive — destroying them would require admitting they matter; the Office instead controls the reading room and staffs it with the captured. Debt closed as governed suppression posture.
- **D-6 disposition (the feast registry):** the Office cannot confiscate, censor, or falsify what it must venerate. Its counter-move is **interpretive capture**: Office-trained feast-clerks staff the Temple registry, indexing it liturgically (by feast, never by interval) so that the dangerous reading — counting the gaps — requires a re-tabulation no pious reader has reason to perform. The registry stays public, sacred, complete, and *illegible in exactly one direction*. Debt closed. (Legibility warning inverted: the state protects itself by keeping a record illegible — the pass's second synthesis-level insight.)
- **Negative space revealing suppression:** no public long-period astronomy chair in a republic otherwise proud of navigation science; a censused state with a four-day-a-decade census hole.

## Synthesis sentence (mandatory)

Because the intercalation of the Founders' Rest changes what a lawful day is, the Office of the Kalends and its Censorate control dating, almanacs, and assertion itself, producing an undated-time economy captured by day-brokers, touts, and conversion clerks, with the Temple's feast registry as unscrubbable sacred evidence — winners: the Office, the brokers, the Temple; losers: the quiet-born, honest trade, and every Censor's conscience; residue: two ages, the noon breath, blank bound pages; conflict: the commons of the count eroding toward the day someone can afford the calendar case.
