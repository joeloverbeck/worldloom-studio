## Date-type separation (the drift, per `09`'s date types)

- **Event date:** Year 0 — the count fixed at 365; drift true from the first year.
- **Discovery date:** Year 36 — the Office's observers measure the nine-day accumulation (S1d).
- **Public date:** never, domestically; abroad, publicly known since roughly Year 60 (foreign astronomers publish; nothing stops them).
- **Institutional date:** Years 43–44 — the Still Season and the first Rest; the Censorate and closing law in the same settlement.
- **Ordinary-life date:** Years ~70–100 — one full generation for Rest etiquette, eve-and-morrow custom, and two-age bookkeeping to become manners rather than policy.
- **Mythic date:** Year 43 backwards-projected — the Rest liturgy teaches that the Founders ordained the Rests from the beginning; the theology *misdates its own institution by 43 years*, and that misdating is itself doctrine (a mythic-date/institutional-date split the protocol's vocabulary named exactly).

## Era design (constraint patterns, not costumes)

1. **Era I — the Innocent Count (Y0–36).** Constraint pattern: the count trusted absolutely; drift real but unmeasured; no concealment machinery. Fossils: earliest civic inscriptions date festivals that now fall wrong against the sun — stone the Office cannot edit (weathered, "restored," or walled over; three famous "restoration" scandals live in stonemasons' guild lore).
2. **Era II — the Held Breath (Y36–43).** The Office knows; the Republic doesn't. Constraint pattern: seven years of internal paralysis — confession collapses the doctrine, correction requires machinery that doesn't exist. Fossil: **the constitutional gap.** The college's unauditable authority dates from these years of improvised secrecy and was never regularized afterward. The gap is not an oversight; it is scar tissue.
3. **Era III — the Settlement (Y43–44).** The Still Season (nine days recovered under a plague story), then the first scheduled Rest, the closing law, the Censorate, the Ringers' subordination — one legislative year building the whole apparatus. Constraint pattern: emergency powers becoming permanent in real time.
4. **Era IV — the Long Quiet (Y44–present).** Steady state; the saturated world of the propagation report. Unresolved contradiction carried by the era (per `09`'s era definition): the Long Count problem, below.

## The Long Count problem (debt D-2, resolved to canon here)

The solar year is not exactly 365¼ days — it is slightly less. One Rest every four years therefore *over-corrects* by roughly a day per 128 years. Since Year 44 the college has had to **silently omit two scheduled Rests** (around Years 172 and 300 — the second omission is recent and current). Consequences admitted as canon (gate run within this pass; ledger addendum below):

- The college's deepest table is not the schedule but the **Long Count** — the correction-of-the-correction, known to perhaps three chairs at a time. Every layer of arithmetic is another secret.
- An omitted Rest is theologically dangerous in the opposite direction from a missed one: the populace *likes* Rests (the debt mercy; the blessing doctrine), and the Temple counts them. A longer-than-usual gap between Rests breeds pious grumbling the Office cannot explain away — "the Founders withhold their Rest" reads as civic ill-omen.
- Heterodox noon-vigil keepers around Year 172 recorded noon *slipping the wrong way* for a generation — their surviving observations circulate abroad as the sun-counters' best evidence that the Rests are corrections (only a corrector would need to skip one).

**Sweep verdict:** the fact that the correction itself needs correcting forever, and can never be admitted at either level, is the world's finest temporal pressure — found entirely by this pass's latency/residue discipline. Recorded on the intercalation capability card as an updated limit (card updated in place per record lifecycle; pointer to this pass).

## Chronology pluralism (per `09`)

Standing counts, deliberately not collapsed: (1) the official count; (2) the sun's count (foreign, sun-counter, heterodox); (3) family day-tallies (private, error-accumulating, sentimental); (4) **the Temple's feast registry** — every Rest is a feast, and the Temple has piously recorded every feast since Year 43. **Sweep catch: the feast registry is a complete public archive of the intercalation history that the Office cannot scrub, because it is sacred and it is the Temple's own.** Anyone who counts the feasts against the years gets the cadence; anyone who notices the Year-172 gap gets the Long Count. The world's most dangerous document is a liturgical calendar. (Assigned: institutional pass must give the Office's posture toward the registry; it cannot confiscate what it must venerate.)

## Timeline card — the Concealment Settlement (Y36–44)

- **Event or era:** Era II–III complex: discovery, paralysis, Still Season, first Rest, the apparatus.
- **Time scope:** exact years (load-bearing: the nine-day arrears fixes Y36 ≈ 9 days × 4 years/day; the arithmetic and the history must agree, and do).
- **Truth layer and status:** objective canon (events); public belief holds plague + ordained-Rests; accepted.
- **Causes:** S1a (drift); patient solstice observation; a real plague scare within living memory to make the story stick.
- **Immediate consequences:** nine days' stopped commerce (near-ruin); the Settlement's five instruments (Rest, closing law, Censorate, subordinated Ringers, licensing).
- **Delayed consequences:** everything in Era IV; the Long Count omissions (Y172, Y300).
- **Latency and lag:** seven years of paralysis (Era II) — the plausible institutional-reaction delay `09` demands; concealment machinery cannot be improvised quickly.
- **Fossils and residue:** constitutional gap; quarantine-law clauses fit for a bodiless plague; restored inscriptions; feast registry; "the Still Season" as idiom.
- **Competing chronologies:** the four counts above.
- **Branch chronology:** none — single continuity.
- **Retcon risk:** the Y36/Y43 arithmetic couples the drift rate, the arrears, and the Settlement date; changing any one re-dates the others. Flagged as the world's most retcon-brittle joint.
