# World Kernel — Carillon (the Unnumbered Days)

Filled from `templates/world_kernel.md` (package v0.8), second field trial. Steward has no authorship stake; world invented for this trial.

## World premise

The three-century Republic of Carillon keeps a constitutionally infallible calendar that is wrong. The Office of the Kalends conceals the drift by secretly inserting days that officially do not exist, and the city has learned to live — trade, sin, marry, and pray — inside the missing time.

## Core promise

The experience of a civilization keeping two sets of books about time itself: bureaucratic dread, quiet complicity, and the uncanny grace of hours that never happened. The reader should feel the drift accumulating like debt.

## Starting scale

City. One city-state (Carillon) and the almanac-dependent hinterland its bells govern. Foreign true-count ports exist as pressure at the border, not as mapped territory. Scale expands only if a fact forces it.

## Genre, tone, and consequence-mode commitments

- Primary genre mode (reader-facing shelf label): institutional fantasy / secret-history.
- Secondary modes: the uncanny; quiet literary dread.
- Primary consequence mode: **material/institutional** — rules-in-use, records, prices, censors, and ordinary-life residue are the proof of coherence.
- Secondary consequence modes: **sacred** (the Calendar's constitutional sanctity; the Unstruck Toll) and **lyrical-symbolic** (unnumbered time as motif: silence, blank pages, the second age everyone privately keeps).
- Tonal boundaries: never whimsical clockpunk; the missing days are lived seriously by people with rent to pay. The Toll is dread-adjacent wonder, not horror.
- What the world must never become by accident: a puzzle-box in which the drift gets "solved," or a farce of bumbling bureaucrats. The Office is competent; that is the problem.

## Foundational facts

Seed facts (thin-start rule and decomposition per `05` to follow; each sentence below knowingly smuggles several facts):

1. **SF-1.** The Founders' Calendar, constitutionally infallible, runs fast against the sun by roughly a quarter-day per year, and has since the founding.
2. **SF-2.** The Office of the Kalends conceals the drift by intercalating **unnumbered days** — days with no date, no bells, no courts, no lawful record — roughly one every four years, announced only by the bells' silence at what should be first toll.
3. **SF-3.** Acts performed on an unnumbered day bear no legal date and therefore no legal existence; a quiet profession of **day-brokers** arranges business that must never have happened.
4. **SF-4.** Doubting the Calendar aloud is sedition. The Office's Censors enforce this; dissident **sun-counters** keep the true count abroad and smuggle almanacs in.
5. **SF-5 (protected).** On every unnumbered day, at what the sun says is noon, every bell in Carillon sounds a single toll that no hand strikes — the **Unstruck Toll**.

## Foundational constraints

- metaphysical limits: time itself is ordinary and continuous; nothing supernatural operates in this world except (protected, unexplained) the Unstruck Toll.
- technological or magical limits: early-modern instrumentation — good enough astronomy to detect the drift, no way to hide the sun itself.
- institutional limits: the Office cannot admit the drift without collapsing the Founders'-infallibility doctrine the Republic's legitimacy rests on; every concealment deepens the commitment.
- recurrence / ritual / symbolic limits: all lawful time is marked by bells; silence is the sound of the unnumbered. The Toll recurs invariantly (every unnumbered day, solar noon, one strike, every bell).
- sacred or preservation limits: the Toll is never explained, never predicted wrongly, never weaponized by the plot.
- geographic limits: the Calendar binds only where Carillon's bells and licensed almanacs reach; foreign ports keep true count.
- temporal limits: the drift is cumulative and arithmetic — roughly a quarter-day per year, so the concealment machinery has a fixed cadence it cannot negotiate with.
- aesthetic limits: registers of ink and bell-bronze; the palette of the unnumbered is silence, shutters, and unlit lamps.

## Initial mysteries and protected effects

- **Why the Founders miscounted** — deliberately undecided, recorded as such (not author-secret, not a puzzle promised to readers). Characters have theories; the world does not adjudicate.
- **The Unstruck Toll** — protected effect: sacred opacity / dread-adjacent wonder. Its regularities are canon (timing, invariance, universality across bells); its cause is outside the explainable. Ledger entry required at admission.

## Primary pressures and initial domains

Pressures (forces driving adaptation):

1. The arithmetic of the drift — every concealment buys four years and deepens the debt.
2. Legitimacy doctrine vs. astronomical fact — the Republic cannot be a little bit infallible.
3. The shadow economy's appetite for undated time.
4. Foreign true-count contact — trade, treaties, and tide-tables that refuse to lie.
5. The Toll — an unassimilable public witness that something is owed.

Domains that feel them first: law/governance, economy/trade, religion/ritual, knowledge institutions (astronomy, archives, almanac licensing), daily life.

## Ordinary-life promise

Everyday residue: everyone keeps two ages — **bell-age** (official) and **sun-age** (lived, whispered); almanacs are licensed and stamped; etiquette forbids asking what anyone did "in the quiet"; landlords argue about whether rent accrues on days that do not exist; children play knock-and-hide games in the silence because no one can lawfully be truant on a day that never happened.

Ordinary-life anchor: **Merla Voss**, a bell-ringer's apprentice, who must learn which mornings not to ring and who keeps a private tally of days she has lived that the Republic says she has not. `05` Phase 6 returns to her day.

## Non-naturalistic kernel prompt

Secondary sacred and lyrical modes declared, so:

- **Mode-equivalent pressure line:** the Toll converts concealment into liturgy — the city cannot decide whether it is accused or blessed, so it ritualizes: shutters latched at the silence, a held breath at solar noon, coins turned face-down after the strike.
- **Recurring motif / taboo / reversal / image / dread / symbolic residue:** the single strike out of silence; blank almanac pages bound into books anyway; the taboo on asking about "the quiet."
- **First audit question:** if the Toll publicly marks every unnumbered day at solar noon, why hasn't it already broken the concealment — everyone in the city hears the proof? (Answer owed at admission: the Office's doctrine must have absorbed the Toll, not survived despite it. Candidate: official theology names the Toll the Founders' own seal — the Republic hears it as confirmation, not accusation. This inversion must be admitted as a fact, not assumed.)
