# Contradiction Report C-1 — The quiet-born

Per `templates/contradiction_report.md`. **Audit trail: unedited after disposition.** Trial sequence marker T1.

## Contradiction statement

- Card 5 (original wording): "Acts performed on a Rest cannot bear a lawful date; undated acts have no standing in court or registry."
- Arena 1 finding (`institutional_suppression_pass.md`): births and deaths occur on Rests (midwifery is sun-work; mortality does not observe the calendar), and Registry clerks systematically improvise morrow-dating for them "because the babies are visibly here" — rules-in-use in permanent violation of the law, because the law as written makes persons legally nonexistent.
- Aggravator (temporal): ~73 unnumbered days over 256 years means thousands of quiet-born across the Republic's history — this is not an edge case but a standing population.

## Affected truth layers

Objective canon (the law) vs. objective canon (demography) — a genuine same-layer incompatibility, not a belief conflict.

## Affected scope

Republic jurisdiction, Year 44–present, every Rest; the Registry as institution.

## Contradiction type

Institutional (a practice exists without the offices/rights needed to govern it), with a temporal aggravator (the first-true test proves the world cannot have lived 256 years with this hole unpatched — see `temporal_pass.md`, sequence-integrity checks).

## Who can notice?

Everyone in-world (the quiet-born are a visible class); the audience immediately; this is a *seed-set* defect, not a diegetic dispute.

## Severity (work scale)

Major.

## Higher-authority material

Demography and the sun-work exemption (propagation report, domain 3–4) outrank the law's convenience: people incontestably exist. The law must bend.

## Mystery / protected-effect relationship

None. No protected effect is touched; `checklists/mystery_preservation.md` not triggered (recorded skip, per its own trigger conditions).

## Repair operation(s), primary first

**Historicize** (primary): the patch must be old — a 256-year-old daily system cannot carry this hole; the repair backfills the **Morrow Rule** as Year-51 law (seven years of quiet-born chaos after the Settlement is a plausible institutional lag, and Era IV inherits the patched system). Component moves: **clarify scope** (the undated-acts rule was too broad; civil-status events are carved out), **institutionalize** (the Rule itself, its Registry procedure).

## Retcon cost

- Continuity: none forward (no scene yet written against the unpatched law); the pre-Y51 chaos becomes usable history (the "first quiet-born" petitions as story material).
- Institutional: Registry gains the morrow-attachment procedure.
- Character: quiet-born elders *predating* Y51 were patched retroactively by the Rule's amnesty clause — except those whose families never petitioned; unpatched quiet-born persist at the margins as a residue class (kept deliberately; the repair does not erase its own history).
- Mystery: none. Aesthetic: strengthens the world (the law's mercy is as bureaucratic as its cruelty). Future: the Rule's wording is new canon surface — *see C-2, which this repair directly caused.*

## Propagation required

New wording propagated through Arena 1 (clerks now lawfully refer civil-status events to morrow-attachment); inheritance law stabilized; "born of the morrow" idiom becomes the visible residue; day-brokers lose nothing yet (property acts still undatable) — noted, and this "yet" is exactly where C-2 detonated.

## Resulting canon status or branch decision

Card 5 updated in place: new wording (second wording) becomes present tense; original wording moved to Card 5 History, marked `superseded`, with pointer to this report. The Morrow Rule as a new institution went back through **admission** (recorded as a moderate row appended to the ledger cluster for this repair — new facts created by a repair use admission vocabulary, per `03`/`13` jurisdiction).

## Notes

The jurisdiction boundary held with no hesitation: repair vocabulary for the incompatibility, admission vocabulary for the new institution it created. The composed-repair field ("primary first") fit naturally — trial 1's F-12 fix confirmed in use.

**Disposition: repair required — executed.**
