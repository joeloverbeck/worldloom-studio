# Capability Card — Intercalation of the Founders' Rest

Per `templates/capability_card.md`. Division of labor per `06`: governance record (layer, status, tags, operations, shock-cone digest) lives on fact card 3 in `fact_cards.md`; this card holds the causal analysis of use. Living record.

## Capability

Insert an unnumbered day into the civil calendar: command city-wide bell silence, close courts and registries, and have the day accepted as the Founders' Rest — repeatably, roughly every four years, without the correction being publicly named as one.

## Users

The Intercalator college of the Office of the Kalends (nine chairs, oathed, self-perpetuating). Falsely claimed by: no one — the capability is unclaimable, since claiming it names the correction. (False *Rests* are another matter; see failure modes.)

## Access requirements

College membership (co-opted, oath-bound, numerate); the observatory instruments and the running solar ledger; operational command of the bell towers via the Ringers' Guild; the closing law (S2b) as legal machinery; the theology (S2e) as social machinery.

## Limits

Cannot intercalate two adjacent days (post–Still Season theological strain: two silences in a row read as calamity, not observance). Cannot defer a due Rest by more than a season (solar noon drifts visibly against the bells; noon-vigil keepers would see). Cannot un-insert a day. Cannot operate beyond earshot and almanac reach (hinterland debt D-3). Cannot predict or command the Toll. Cannot hold the four-year cadence forever: the cadence over-corrects by about a day per 128 years, so the college must occasionally omit a scheduled Rest — the Long Count (amended in place per `03` record lifecycle; see `temporal_pass.md` and ledger rows T-1/T-2).

## Failure modes

- **Missed Rest:** drift becomes visible to vigil-keepers and fishermen first — heterodox quarters notice before loyal ones, inverting the Office's information advantage.
- **Leaked schedule:** foreknowledge of a Rest is tradable (see Economy). A leak does not expose the doctrine, but it corrupts the college's monopoly and creates datable patterns a clever clerk could correlate.
- **False Rest:** silencing the bells off-schedule — the gravest conceivable crime (a usurpation of time itself); also the definitive test of the Toll (a false Rest would receive no noon toll — the one experiment nobody dares run, recorded as ledger boundary pressure).
- **Arithmetic residue:** the quarter-day is approximate; the college's own tables accumulate error over centuries (debt D-2 → temporal pass, the Long Count problem).

## Costs

One day of commerce, wages, court time, and tax stamps per Rest — a real recurring price the whole city pays. College side: lifelong confinement of its members' publishable work (an Intercalator can never be a famous astronomer). Political: every Rest is a small loyalty test the Republic must keep winning.

## Countermeasures

Foreign chanceries keep true count and bank the leverage. Sun-counters publish abroad and smuggle. Merchants privately settle by "eve and morrow" custom to route around the dead day. Insurers price Rest-adjacent risk (a cargo that must move on a possible-Rest week costs more). Noon-vigil piety — outwardly devotion, functionally drift-detection — spreads exactly where trust in the Office is lowest.

## Institutions

Teaches: the college (secretly). Regulates: itself — the constitutional gap (who intercalates the Intercalators?) is a named pressure. Blesses: the Temple of the Founders (junior partner, believes its own liturgy). Certifies: the almanac stamp office (S4b). Punishes around it: the Censorate (Card 6). Exploits: day-brokers (S3b) sell the dead day's darkness.

## Economy

Valuable: foreknowledge of the schedule (the leak market — "calendar touts" selling Rest-eve tips to bakers, shippers, and brokers, assigned to pass `12`); Rest-eve settlement services; count-conversion expertise (S4d). Cheap: nothing — the Rest removes a day of supply citywide (small recurring price ripples: Rest-eve bread costs more). Illegal: dated records of Rests; unlicensed ephemerides. Counterfeit: forged almanac stamps; false day-brokers selling fake "quiet" appointments on numbered days to out-of-towners.

## Distribution

Common knowledge as *rite*; monopolized as *practice*; understood as *correction* by the college, foreign chanceries, sun-counters, and an unknowable fringe of silent arithmeticians the Censorate manufactures by design (Card 6 limit).

## Repeated-use test

~64 uses to date plus the Still Season: the hundred-use question is the world's actual history, and the answer is saturation — the audit's mature-residue shopping list (two-age bookkeeping, Rest etiquette, broker cant, eve-and-morrow settlement custom, vigil piety) *is* the repeated-use record. Forward version: another century of use meets the Long Count problem (D-2) — the arithmetic eventually demands either a second Still Season or a cadence change, and the college knows it.

## Aesthetic residue

The held breath at solar noon on a Rest; coins turned face-down after the Toll; blank bound pages in every ledger ("the quiet has its pages"); the Ringers' Guild oath ("I ring what is numbered"); bell-bronze as the metal of honesty in proverbs; the pitying idiom "born of the morrow."
