## Card 1 — The quarter-day drift (S1a)

**Fact statement.** The Founders' Calendar year is exactly 365 days. The solar year is about 365 and a quarter days. Civil dates therefore run ahead of the sun by a quarter-day each year, and have since the founding.

**Working scope.** Continuity: baseline. Era: all. Place: everywhere the Calendar is used. Population: all.

**Fact type.** Constraint / metaphysical-grade natural rule. **Admission level 4** (world premise) → foundational work owed.

**Consequence mode.** Material/institutional (the drift is arithmetic, not magic).

**Truth layer.** Objective canon. **Status.** Accepted. **Constraint tags.** None — physics carries no tags.

**Prerequisites.** Hard: none (it is bedrock). Epistemic: detectability requires only patient solstice observation — this low epistemic floor is what makes the concealment problem hard, and it is a feature.

**Costs and limits.** The drift cannot be bribed, deferred, or suppressed — only corrected or lied about. Everything in the world flows from that pair of options.

**Shock-cone summary.** Digest: all consequences of S1a travel through the intercalation system — see `propagation_report_S2a.md` (master record).

**Evidence and belief.** The sun is the evidence, daily, for anyone who counts. The Office knows; foreign astronomers know; sun-counters publish it abroad; the populace mostly believes the doctrine (Card 2).

**Contradiction risks.** None internally; it is the ground other facts must not contradict. The standing tension with Card 2 is layered (objective fact vs. official doctrine), not a canon contradiction.

**Mystery / wonder risks.** None. The drift is deliberately mundane — the world's one non-mundane thing is the Toll, and it must stay the only one.

**Admission decision operation.** Accept.

**Notes.** Kept exact (365 vs ~365¼) so the intercalation cadence is checkable arithmetic. Residual error beyond the quarter-day approximation: named as debt D-2, resolved by the temporal pass (see `temporal_pass.md` — the Long Count problem).

## Card 2 — The Doctrine of the Perfect Count (S1b)

**Fact statement.** The Constitution declares the Founders' Calendar infallible. Official doctrine holds the count perfect; no organ of the Republic may entertain evidence against it, and acts premised on its error are void.

**Working scope.** Continuity: baseline. Era: since founding. Place: Republic jurisdiction. Population: all subjects; binding on courts, registries, schools, pulpits.

**Fact type.** Law / doctrine. **Admission level 3** (institution) → major work owed.

**Consequence mode.** Material/institutional primary; sacred secondary (the doctrine is liturgical as well as legal).

**Truth layer.** Objective canon *that the doctrine exists and binds*. The doctrine's content is an official **lie** at the objective layer — tracked per `03`: who knows (Office college, foreign chanceries, sun-counters), who benefits (the Republic's legitimacy, everyone invested in it), what evidence exists (the sky; the Office's own Year-36 measurements, D-4), what exposure would do (regime-legitimacy crisis, not merely embarrassment).

**Constraint tags.** Sacred, era-bound.

**Prerequisites.** Institutional: a founding myth strong enough to make infallibility constitutional rather than merely official. Epistemic: mass innumeracy about long-period astronomy — schooling must actively produce it (licensed curriculum; feeds Card 6).

**Costs and limits.** The doctrine cannot be a little bit wrong: any concession collapses it. This brittleness is the world's central institutional constraint — the Office cannot ever choose a small honest correction.

**Shock-cone summary.** Digest of `propagation_report_S2a.md`: the doctrine is why correction must wear ritual clothes (Rests), why assertion is sedition (Card 6), why almanacs are licensed (S4b), and why foreign leverage exists (S4d).

**Evidence and belief.** Public belief: sincere, majority. Local belief: dockside and fishing families are quietly heterodox (tide-tables do not lie). Elite belief: split between cynics and true believers *inside the Office itself* — the college contains members who believe the Rests genuinely are ordained and the arithmetic merely describes the Founders' plan. (This intra-Office schism is the card's contribution to faction texture; the institutional pass develops it.)

**Contradiction risks.** With S1a by design (layered, managed). With any future fact that lets a court weigh astronomical evidence — flagged for Card 6's boundary.

**Mystery / wonder risks.** The doctrine must never explain the Toll away or claim to predict it beyond the Rests — the Toll belongs to the mystery ledger, and doctrine touches it only as the recorded propaganda claim S2e.

**Admission decision operation.** Accept; institutionalize (courts, schools, pulpits as enforcement organs) as component.

**Notes.** The gate's enemy-response box produced the intra-Office believer/cynic split — the world's most useful faction line, entirely generated by asking who *inside* the sponsoring institution believes the propaganda.

## Card 3 — The intercalation of the Founders' Rest (S2a)

**Fact statement.** Since Year 44, the Office of the Kalends intercalates one unnumbered day — a Rest — roughly every four years, keeping civil dates within a day of the sun. Rests are public and ritualized; the schedule and its arithmetic are Office secrets; the practice's corrective purpose is known inside the college and denied everywhere else.

**Working scope.** Continuity: baseline. Era: Year 44–present. Place: Carillon and bell-governed hinterland. Population: all; operated by the Intercalator college.

**Fact type.** Capability + institutional practice. **Admission level 4** (this is the premise machinery) → foundational work owed.

**Consequence mode.** Material/institutional primary; sacred secondary (the Rest is a rite whatever else it is).

**Truth layer.** Objective canon (the practice); author-secret→elite (its purpose). **Status.** Accepted with constraints. **Constraint tags.** Monopolized, secret (schedule and arithmetic), ritual.

**Prerequisites.** Hard: the Intercalator college (instruments, succession, oath); bell-tower command over the city; the closing law (S2b). Soft: the theology (S2e) that makes silence read as observance. Temporal: the Still Season (Card 4) had to clear the arrears first.

**Costs and limits.** Each Rest costs the city a day of commerce, court time, and wages — the Republic pays a real, recurring price to maintain its lie, and everyone feels it without being allowed to name it. The college cannot intercalate adjacent days (theological strain after the Still Season) nor skip a due Rest by more than a season (the sun tells).

**Shock-cone summary.** Pointer: `propagation_report_S2a.md` is the master record (full 14-domain pass).

**Evidence and belief.** See capability card (`capability_card_intercalation.md`) for the causal analysis of use — access, failure modes, countermeasures, leak economy. Cross-reference per `06`'s division of labor: this card holds governance; that card holds use.

**Contradiction risks.** The schedule-secrecy tag composes dangerously with S2d (silence as only signal): anyone with a noon-vigil habit detects Rests independently. Checked at gate; absorbed — vigil-keeping is itself visible and therefore governable (Card 6's Censors watch the watchers).

**Mystery / wonder risks.** The Toll rides on Rests; nothing in this card may predict or explain the Toll. Boundary respected: the college schedules Rests, and the Toll *answers* — the card records the correlation as canon and assigns its meaning to the ledger.

**Admission decision operation.** Accept; constrain (secrecy), institutionalize (the college), price (the lost day) as components.

**Notes.** Gate insight: the "who can counterfeit it" box → a false Rest (silencing the bells off-schedule) is the world's gravest conceivable crime — instant candidate for the institutional pass's suppression analysis, and the reason bell-ringers (Merla) are an oathed, watched profession.

## Card 4 — The Still Season (S2c)

**Fact statement.** In Year 43 the Republic recovered nine accumulated days of drift in one stroke: nine consecutive unnumbered days, publicly declared a plague quarantine. The quarantine story is official history; the recovery is the Office's founding secret operation.

**Working scope.** Continuity: baseline. Era: Year 43 (event), residue to present. Place: Carillon. Population: all (memory), Office (truth).

**Fact type.** Event / history. **Admission level 3** → major work owed (ancient fact → heavy residue owed per the admission maxim).

**Consequence mode.** Material/institutional; the event also anchors the sacred calendar (the Rest liturgy's forms descend from Still Season improvisations).

**Truth layer.** Objective canon (event); **public belief** holds the plague story (an engineered honest-error-by-now: the original tellers lied, its inheritors believe). **Status.** Accepted. **Constraint tags.** Era-bound; secret (true reason).

**Prerequisites.** A real plague scare within living memory of Year 43 to make the story stick; emergency-quarantine law predating the event.

**Costs and limits.** Nine days of stopped commerce nearly broke the city — the event must read as trauma, not administrative trick.

**Shock-cone summary.** Residues (per `06` step 16): quarantine law still contains clauses that only make sense for a plague with no bodies; "the Still Season" as idiom for any dead stretch of business; leases and apprenticeship terms inherited from Year 43 that count "the lost nine" explicitly; a burial-registry anomaly (no mortality spike inside a nine-day quarantine) sitting in the archives as standing evidence debt (D-4); the eldest families' private day-counts all disagree with the civil count by the same nine days.

**Evidence and belief.** The anomaly is findable by any clerk with patience and access — which is why archive access is licensed (feeds pass `12`).

**Contradiction risks.** The plague story must never acquire canonical bodies: if any later fact names Still Season dead, the cover story hardens into canon and the anomaly evaporates. Flagged as a standing authorial guard.

**Mystery / wonder risks.** Toll history: the first Unstruck Toll sounded on the first Still day — recorded in the mystery ledger as fixed regularity, not explained here.

**Admission decision operation.** Accept; historicize (it is the world's path-dependence anchor) as component.

## Card 5 — The undated-acts law (S3a) — supersession chain carrier

**Fact statement (current, third wording).** Acts performed on a Rest cannot bear a lawful date and have no standing in court or registry, and pleading a Rest-day act is itself an offense against the closing law — **except** that births, deaths, marriages, and adoptions occurring on a Rest attach by law to the following numbered day *if and only if* declared on that morrow at the Registry's **Blind Window**, which records the event as "of the morrow" and records nothing else. Undeclared events remain undatable forever; no other class of act may claim morrow-attachment on any argument.

**Working scope.** Continuity: baseline. Era: current wording since Year 68 (see History). Place: Republic jurisdiction. Population: all.

**Fact type.** Law. **Admission level 3** → major. Current wording entered under **Level 5** discipline (repair of established canon).

**Consequence mode.** Material/institutional.

**Truth layer.** Objective canon. **Status.** Accepted with constraints. **Constraint tags.** Jurisdictional, geography-bound (Carillon law; foreign courts date as they please — the conversion trade lives in that gap).

**Prerequisites.** S2b (the closing law); the Registry; the Blind Window office (admitted Year 68 — see `contradiction_report_C2.md` and its admission row below).

**Costs and limits.** The Blind Window's blindness is load-bearing: it may not record hour, witnesses, or circumstance, or it becomes a dated record of a Rest and violates S2b. Its clerks are oathed like bell-ringers.

**Shock-cone summary.** Digest: the law creates the undated economy (S3b/S3c) as its shadow; the Morrow-Rule exception channels the one intolerable harm (undatable persons) through a single controlled aperture; everything else stays dark by design. Master analysis: `propagation_report_S2a.md` §law/governance plus both contradiction reports.

**Evidence and belief.** Public: everyone knows the Blind Window; "born of the morrow" is a common, faintly pitying idiom. The pre-C1 era's undatable elders ("the quiet-born") persist as a living residue class — their status was *not* retroactively repaired (see C-1 disposition).

**Contradiction risks.** History below records two cashed risks (C-1, C-2). Remaining: foreign-jurisdiction dating of Rest-day acts (a Carillon Rest-day contract signed aboard a foreign ship in harbor is datable under that flag) — named debt, assigned to a future spatial/diplomacy pass.

**Mystery / wonder risks.** None; deliberately mundane law.

**Admission decision operation.** Accept (original, Year —); current wording entered via repair operations, see History.

**Repair operation, if any.** C-1: historicize + institutionalize (the Morrow Rule, backfilled as Year-51 law). C-2: clarify scope + add constraint + institutionalize (the Blind Window). Details in the two contradiction reports; new institutions created by each repair went back through admission separately (ledger addenda in each report).

**Notes.** This card is the trial's record-lifecycle exercise: two repairs, two supersessions, card updated in place both times. Reading experience recorded in the trial log.

**History.**

1. **Original wording (superseded, retired by `contradiction_report_C1.md`, trial sequence marker T1):** "Acts performed on a Rest cannot bear a lawful date; undated acts have no standing in court or registry." — Superseded because it made persons born or dying on a Rest legally nonexistent (C-1); replaced by wording admitting the Morrow Rule.
2. **Second wording (superseded, retired by `contradiction_report_C2.md`, trial sequence marker T2):** "Acts performed on a Rest cannot bear a lawful date and have no standing in court or registry, except that acts affecting the civil status of persons attach by law to the following numbered day (the Morrow Rule, Year 51)." — Superseded because "acts affecting the civil status of persons" proved exploitable (creditors and quiet-marriage brokers dated Rest-day acts through the civil-status argument, breaking the tolerance equilibrium S3c); replaced by the current enumerated-and-windowed wording.

## Card 6 — The Censorate and the sedition boundary (S4a, promoted from ledger row during pass `12`)

**Fact statement.** Publicly asserting that the Calendar is in error is sedition, tried before the Office's Censors. The boundary is precise: *practicing astronomy is lawful and licensed; publishing or asserting the forbidden conclusion is the crime.* The Censors' working product is not secrecy but unaffordability — they keep the cost of public assertion above what any speaker can pay.

**Working scope.** Continuity: baseline. Era: since Year 44 (statute), practice evolved. Place: Republic. Population: all speakers; licensed astronomers under special scrutiny.

**Fact type.** Law + institution. **Admission level 3** → major (promoted; original minor row retained in `admission_ledger.md` with promotion note).

**Consequence mode.** Material/institutional.

**Truth layer.** Objective canon. **Status.** Accepted. **Constraint tags.** Sacred, dangerous.

**Prerequisites.** Card 2 (the doctrine it protects); a licensing regime for astronomy (navigation and agriculture cannot be banned); informers' economy.

**Costs and limits.** The Censorate cannot prosecute arithmetic done silently, noon vigils, or private counts — only assertion. This limit is why the city is full of people who *know* and the crime remains rare: the law manufactures private knowledge and public silence, which is the world's tonal signature.

**Shock-cone summary.** See `institutional_suppression_pass.md` (master record for this card's arena analysis, informant economy, and counter-institution response).

**Evidence and belief.** Conviction requires two witnesses to the assertion — which makes the two-witness meeting the dangerous unit of dissent, and pairs of friends the natural cell structure of the sun-counters' domestic sympathizers.

**Contradiction risks.** With foreign diplomats (immune speakers) — absorbed: chancery practice is to never assert, only to *invoice* (conversion fees say what words may not). With S4c's smuggled sun-tables: possession vs. assertion distinction holds (possession is contraband under S4b, not sedition under S4a).

**Mystery / wonder risks.** The Censors must never prosecute Toll interpretation (heresy about the Toll is the theologians' turf, not sedition) — boundary keeps the mystery ledger's jurisdiction clean.

**Admission decision operation.** Accept; institutionalize; price (the unaffordability doctrine) as components. Promotion recorded per the ledger's Promotions rule.

**Notes.** Promotion trigger: during pass `12`, three facts (S2d leak market, S3c equilibrium, S4c smuggling) all turned out to depend on the Censorate's enforcement posture — "load-bearing" per the Promotions rule, so the row became this card.
