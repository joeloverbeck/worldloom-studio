Two entries per `templates/mystery_ledger_entry.md`. Entry 2 tests whether the ledger scales down to a small authorial boundary.

## Entry 1 — The Unstruck Toll (S5a–S5b)

**Protected effect type.** Sacred opacity / wonder hybrid (dread-adjacent, not horror).

**Puzzle question, if any.** None. The Toll is not a mystery to be solved; it is a fact the world cannot digest.

**Mystery type, if applicable.** Not applicable (no puzzle).

**What is fixed.** On every Rest, at solar noon, every **hung bell within the walls** sounds exactly one toll no hand strikes; unhung bronze is silent. It has never missed a Rest, never doubled, never sounded on a numbered day, never varied in count. It struck first on the first day of the Still Season. It follows the *true* solar noon, not the bells' official time — the Toll keeps the sun's count, publicly, four times a decade. Bells removed from the city do not toll; bells newly hung do. *(Regularity's object clarified in place at QA — see `qa_regression_profile.md`, repair loop; `checklists/mystery_preservation.md` run for the touch; cause boundary untouched. Consequence gained: bell-muffling as quiet impiety; edge-testing bell-hangers as a governed fringe.)*

**What is author-secret.** Nothing. The author-secret set is deliberately empty: the steward does not know what the Toll is, and deciding would itself breach the design (same disposition trial 1 reached for the Weeping Shelf; adopted here on its merits — the Toll must be larger than the authorial model, or the world's central irony collapses into a gimmick).

**What is deliberately undecided.** Whether the Toll is response, coincidence-shaped law, witness, or judgment. Whether it "knows" the schedule or the sun. What a false Rest would receive.

**What is forbidden.** Any mechanism, agent, or physics for the Toll. Any use of the Toll to convey information beyond its fixed regularities (it never varies, therefore it cannot signal). Any scene in which the Toll resolves a plot problem. Any canonical answer to the false-Rest experiment — the experiment may be feared, proposed, even attempted in-story, but its outcome is inside the boundary.

**Truth-layer boundaries.** Objective canon: the regularities above. Mystery boundary: the cause. Propaganda: the Founders'-seal doctrine (S2e). Local belief: dockside "the sun rings for its own" heterodoxy. Elite belief: the college's private terror that the Toll is an audit. Disputed claim: whether the Toll was heard outside the walls (testimony conflicts; deliberately kept disputed).

**Preservation boundary.** The Toll must remain larger than every institution that speaks about it. The Republic's most powerful body schedules days around a thing it cannot explain and dares not test — that asymmetry is the world's sacred center.

**Who knows partial truth.** Everyone hears it; no one knows more than the regularities. The college knows the sharpest version (it tracks true noon — the Toll is *more accurate than the Office*). The Temple knows its doctrine is reverse-engineered.

**False beliefs.** Founders'-seal orthodoxy (engineered, S2e); "the bells mourn the unnumbered dead" (folk); "foreign bells toll too, hushed up" (rumor — false, and its falseness is canon: the Toll is Carillon's alone).

**Evidence available.** Two and a half centuries of invariance; the Still Season first-strike record; ringers' guild testimony (ropes still, clappers moving — or not moving; guild accounts conflict and are allowed to).

**Evidence missing.** Any observation of a bell *between* silence and strike; any Toll under a false Rest; any Toll beyond the walls. Cannot be safely sought: watching a bell at noon on a Rest is lawful but proverbially unlucky, and the guild forbids its own members the vigil.

**Evidence habitat.** Bell towers, guild oral memory, Temple doctrine, dockside song, the college's sealed noon-ledgers.

**Consequences already visible.** The coin-turning gesture; the held breath; Rest-day noon as the city's one involuntary shared moment; the college's schedule discipline (a missed Rest would face the question of whether the Toll comes anyway — dread as compliance mechanism); Temple revenue from seal-doctrine feast days; heterodox vigil piety.

**Recurrence / motif / transformation.** One strike out of silence, four times a decade, forever — invariance *is* the motif. Transformation is forbidden to the effect and reserved to its interpreters: doctrines, songs, and fears change around a thing that never does.

**Reveal permissions.** None, in any branch or medium. Regularities may be dramatized freely.

**Reveal prohibitions.** Everything causal. Adaptations may not add a glimpsed striker, a resonance theory, or an ancient machine.

**Explanation-pressure operation.** Consecrate (primary); forbid (adaptations).

**What would break if solved or flattened.** The world's central irony — a state lying about time while time, or something, publicly keeps the true count — becomes a puzzle-box answer key. The Office's dread evaporates; the theology becomes merely wrong instead of tragically improvised; the ordinary residue (held breath, turned coins) loses its object.

**Preservation checks.** Consequences visible ✓ (list above). Fixed/secret/undecided/forbidden separated ✓. Not hiding an ordinary contradiction ✓ (the Toll resolves nothing; it *creates* pressure). Not converted into a puzzle ✓ (puzzle question: none). No reveal contemplated ✓.

### Sacred-opacity accountability

- **Refused explanation:** the Toll's cause, agency, and meaning.
- **Consequences still fixed:** timing (true solar noon of every Rest), count (one), scope (every bell within the walls; hung bells join, removed bells leave), history (first strike on the first Still day), and its social machinery (gesture, doctrine, dread) — all auditable.
- **Recurrence / boundary rule:** absolute invariance distinguishes the earned effect from convenience — the Toll can never do anything *new* for the plot; an effect that cannot vary cannot be cashed in.
- **Contradiction implications:** any future fact giving the Toll a mechanism, a variance, or an extramural echo requires repair; the false-Rest outcome must never be canonized.
- **Critique still allowed:** clarity of the regularities, tonal fit, whether invariance is maintained across scenes, whether interpreter factions stay plausible, audience trust in the no-reveal contract.
- **Legitimate-vs-cheat note:** earned because the opacity closes no plot conveniently and *costs* the powerful (the Office must live under an unexplainable public witness); a cheat would use the Toll to signal, judge, or rescue. It is load-bearing dread, not protected vagueness: every regularity invites audit, only the cause is refused.

## Entry 2 — The Founders' Count (S1c)

**Protected effect type.** Governed mystery (authorial boundary).

**Puzzle question, if any.** Why did the Founders fix the year at 365 days?

**Mystery type.** Authorial mystery boundary; historical/motive.

**What is fixed.** The count was fixed at the founding; the drift is real (Card 1); the Office measured it by Year 36 (S1d).

**What is author-secret.** Nothing.

**What is deliberately undecided.** Error, haste, ignorance, or intent — the world does not adjudicate the Founders' motive. Undecidability is design: every faction's theory of the Founders is a self-portrait (the cynics' Founders were pragmatists, the believers' Founders left a test, the sun-counters' Founders were simply wrong), and deciding would collapse the faction ecology into right and wrong parties.

**What is forbidden.** Canonical evidence of motive: no founding diary, no deathbed confession, no unambiguous document may enter canon. Documents *claiming* motive are welcome as diegetic claims, forgeries, and propaganda.

**Truth-layer boundaries.** Objective: count, drift, Year-36 measurement. Deliberately undecided: motive. Everything characters say about motive: diegetic claim / propaganda / mythic truth as tagged case by case.

**Preservation boundary / recurrence / reveals.** The undecided motive must keep generating conflicting self-portraits; no reveal in any branch. Explanation-pressure operation: forbid.

**What would break if solved.** The believer/cynic schism inside the Office (Card 2's best texture) would resolve; the doctrine's tragedy (defending a possibly innocent mistake as sacred perfection) depends on nobody knowing.

**Preservation checks.** All pass; this entry is deliberately not being used to dodge a consequence — the drift's consequences are fully governed regardless of motive.
