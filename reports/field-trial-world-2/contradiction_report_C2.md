## Contradiction statement

- Card 5 (second wording, from C-1): "…except that acts affecting the civil status of persons attach by law to the following numbered day (the Morrow Rule, Year 51)."
- S3b/S3c (admission ledger): the day-broker economy and the Office's tolerance equilibrium depend on Rest-day acts being *reliably* undatable.
- The collision: "acts affecting the civil status of persons" is an open class. Two exploits follow within a generation of Y51 (backfilled history, consistent with the era structure): (a) **quiet marriages** — marriages contracted on Rests *for deniability* become datable on the morrow whenever one party later finds attachment convenient, converting the brokers' flagship deniability product into a one-sided trap; (b) **creditor suits** — creditors argue that a Rest-day debt acknowledgment "affects the civil status" of the debtor (solvency, standing to contract), and sympathetic courts begin dating settlements. Both make the same act datable-or-not depending on who argues — the tolerance equilibrium (S3c) breaks, morrow courts flood, and the Office faces the thing it cannot survive: routine judicial dating of the quiet.
