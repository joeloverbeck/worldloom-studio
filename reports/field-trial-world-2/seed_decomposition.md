# Seed Decomposition — Carillon

Per `05` Phase 2. Granularity rule applied: split until each seed could be independently rejected without destroying its siblings; stop when further division produces facts too small to owe consequences.

**Design consequence of decomposition (recorded):** SF-2 as written ("conceals the drift by intercalating unnumbered days") failed its own decomposition — a whole city cannot fail to notice a day. The concealment cannot be of the days' *existence*; it must be of their *reason*. The unnumbered days are therefore public and ritualized (the **Founders' Rest**); the Office's secret is the arithmetic. What is seditious is not observing the Rest but calling it a correction. The kernel's first-audit-question answer (the Toll absorbed as the Founders' seal) slots into this as engineered public belief. Kernel wording of SF-2/SF-4 is refined accordingly; kernel essence unchanged.

## SF-1 — the drift and the doctrine

- **S1a.** The Founders' Calendar year is exactly 365 days; the solar year is about 365¼ days; civil dates therefore run ahead of the sun by a quarter-day per year. *(foundational constraint; objective canon)*
- **S1b.** The Constitution declares the Founders' Calendar infallible; official doctrine holds the count perfect. *(law/doctrine; at the objective layer this doctrine is false — an official lie)*
- **S1c.** Why the Founders fixed the year at 365 is deliberately undecided (error, haste, or intent — the world does not adjudicate). *(mystery posture, authorial-boundary type)*
- **S1d.** By Year 36 of the Republic, the Office of the Kalends' own observers had measured the accumulated nine-day drift. *(historical fact)*

## SF-2 — the concealment machinery (refined: the Founders' Rest)

- **S2a.** Since Year 44, the Office of the Kalends intercalates one unnumbered day (**a Rest**) approximately every four years, keeping civil dates within a day of the sun. *(repeatable capability + institutional practice; the schedule arithmetic is an Office secret)*
- **S2b.** A Rest has no date: no bells (save the Toll), courts and registries closed, and making a dated record of a Rest is forbidden. *(law)*
- **S2c.** The nine days of arrears were recovered in Year 43 by the **Still Season**: nine consecutive unnumbered days, officially a plague quarantine. *(historical trauma; residue)*
- **S2d.** A Rest is announced only by the bells' silence at first toll; the schedule is never published. *(information control)*
- **S2e.** Official theology teaches the Rests as the **Founders' Rest** — an ordained observance, not a correction — and teaches the Unstruck Toll as the Founders' seal upon it. *(engineered public belief; belief layer, not objective canon)*

## SF-3 — the undated economy

- **S3a.** Acts performed on a Rest cannot bear a lawful date; undated acts have no standing in court or registry. *(law; the load-bearing legal fact of the world)*
- **S3b.** **Day-brokers** arrange transactions, meetings, and settlements on Rests for clients who need deniability; the profession is unlicensed but tolerated. *(profession/economy)*
- **S3c.** The Office tolerates the Rest-day economy because prosecuting it would require officially dating the undatable. *(suppression equilibrium)*

## SF-4 — enforcement and counter-count

- **S4a.** Publicly asserting the Calendar is in error is sedition, enforced by the Office's Censors. *(law/institution)*
- **S4b.** Almanacs must be licensed and stamped by the Office; unlicensed ephemerides are contraband. *(law/economy)*
- **S4c.** Exiled **sun-counters** maintain the true count abroad and smuggle sun-tables into the city. *(counter-institution)*
- **S4d.** Foreign ports date documents by their own counts; conversion between counts is a skilled, politically sensitive trade. *(trade/diplomacy residue)*

## SF-5 — the protected effect

- **S5a.** On every Rest at solar noon, every bell within Carillon sounds exactly one toll that no hand strikes. *(protected effect; its regularities are canon, its cause is protected)*
- **S5b.** The Toll has never missed a Rest, never doubled, and never sounded on a numbered day. *(invariance boundary of the protected effect)*

## Count and granularity check

18 atomic seeds from 5 kernel sentences. Each passes independent rejection: e.g., rejecting S2e (the theology) leaves the intercalation machinery intact but changes the suppression problem; rejecting S3b (brokers) leaves S3a as law without a profession exploiting it. None is too small to owe consequences (S2d owes information-control consequences; S5b owes observation-attempt consequences).
